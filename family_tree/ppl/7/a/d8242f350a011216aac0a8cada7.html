==Biography=='''Alan la Zuche alias la Suche''',<ref name="CalInqPMH3Vol1p234">{{EE citation |author=The Deputy Keeper of the Records |title=''Calendar of Inquisitions Post Mortem and other Analogous Documents preserved in the Public Record Office'' |publication-place=London |publisher=His Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department |volume= Vol I Henry III |year=1904|accessdate=27 September 2014|url=https://archive.org/stream/calendarinquisi00offigoog#page/n293/mode/2up|repository=archive.org|pages=234}} Abstract No Alan la Zuche alias la Suche.</ref> '''de la Zouche or Zouch, Baron Zouche'''<ref name="dnbStanley414">{{EE citation|title=''Dictionary of national biography''|editor1-last=Sidney|editor1-first=Lee|year=1900|publication-place=London|publisher=Smith, Elder, & Co.|volume=Vol. LXIII Wordsworth&mdash;Zuylestein|repository=Archive.org|url=https://archive.org/stream/dictionaryofnati63stepuoft#page/414/mode/2up|pages=414-5}}</ref> '''la Zusche'''<ref name="PatentsHenIIIp70">{{EE citation|editor1-last= Assistant Keeper of HM Public Records|title=''Calendar of the Patent Rolls Preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=London|publisher=His Majesty's Stationery Office|volume=Henry III 1247-1258|year=1908|accessdate=10 October 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/patentrollsreig00unkngoog#page/n82/mode/2up|pages=70}}</ref>
Alan La Zouche was born about 1203 in Ashby-De-La-Zouch, Leicestershire, England.
Alan was the oldest son of Roger de la Zouch and his wife Margaret.<ref name="MLZouche">Zouche: Untitled English Nobility T-Z, Medieval Lands, http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/ENGLISHNOBILITYMEDIEVAL3T-Z.htm#_Toc389115910, accessed 27 September 2014.</ref> Alan was the grandson of Alan de la Zouch and his wife, Adeliza or Alice de Belmeis, heir of the house of Belmeis, her inheritance included Tong Castle in Shropshire, Ashby or Ashby de la Zouch in Leicestershire, and North Molton in Devonshire.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> His grandfather's older brother, Eudes de Porhoet, was the count of Brittany, although his right to hold the title was disputed.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> His grandfather's uncle, also named Alan, was the founder of the viscounty of Rohan.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />
Alan's father Roger succeeded his brother William de la Zouch to the family's estates.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> In 1 Henry III [1216-7], Henry III, King of England, granted the forfeited estates of Roger's kinsmen, the viscounts of Rohan, to Roger as a reward for his loyalty to the king's father, King John.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> Roger died before 3 November, 1238.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />

Alan had siblings:
#William la Zouche, married to Maud;<ref name="MLZouche" />
#Alice, who was married to William de Harcourt;<ref name="MLZouche" />
#Lora, who was married to Gilbert de Stanford;<ref name="MLZouche" />#Eon, who was married to Millicent de Cauntelo, widow of John de Mohaut;<ref name="MLZouche" />
Alan married Elena de Quincy (died 1269)<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> before 1242 in Winchester, Hampshire, England. Elena was the daughter and coheir of Roger de Quincy, earl of Winchester,<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> and his first wife Helen of Galloway.<ref name="MLZouche" /> In 1267 Alan succeeded to her share of the Qunicy estates.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />

Alan and Elena had children:#Roger de la Zouch, born 1240/42,<ref name="CalInqPMH3Vol1p234" /> died in 1285, who was summoned to parliament, married Ela, the daughter of Stephen Longuespee and his wife Emelina or Emmeline de Rydeleford, countess of Ulster;<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> <ref name="MLZouche" />

===Career===Alan was summoned on 15 June 1242 to attend the king with horses and arms in Gascony.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />
On 2 July 1250, Alan la Zusche was appointed justice of Chester, to the keeping of all the lands in the county of Chester and of North Wales, including: Ros, Rewennoc, Defferencloyd, and Englefeus, to for two years and one quarter, commencing on the morrow of the Translation of St Thomas the Martyr, in 34 Henry III, he was replacing John de Grey, and Walkelin de Ardern, William de Boydel, Geoffrey de Dutton and Robert Patric were to go personnalyy to the castles of Gannoc and Dissard, and the king's castles and manors to see what state they were in and how they were munitioned, and they are to write a report, chirographed between them and the said J, one part to the justice, and the other under their seals to the king.<ref name="PatentsHenIIIp70" />
Alan served as deputy to Edward, prince of Wales and earl of Chester, and in 1255 was appointed justice of Ireland under Edward.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> Alan adhered to the king during the baron's wars.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> He was appointed sheriff of Northamptonshire on 9 July 1261, at times ignoring the provisions of Magna Carta by acting as justice itinerant in his own shire and in Buckinghamshire and Hampshire.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> In 1261 he was justice of the forests south of Trent.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> In 1262 he held forest pleas at Worcester.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> In 1263 he was appointed king's seneschal.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> After peace was declared between Henry III and Gilbert de Clare, eighth earl of Gloucester, Alan was appointed warden of London and constable of the Tower on 23 or 25 June 1267 and held this office until Easter, 1268.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />

===Death=== On 19 June, 1270, a suit was being heard before the justices in ''banco'' at Westminster Hall, filed by Alan against John de Warrenne, Earl Warenne, which Alan appeared to be winning, when he and his son, Roger, were murderously attacked by the Earl and his followers.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> <ref>Richardson, Douglas, ''Magna Carta Ancestries'', 2005</ref> On 4 August, Warenne made his peace and agreed to pay a substantial compensation to the Zouches.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />

Alan died on 10 August, 1270.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />
An ''Inquisition'' taken on the Sunday before St Michael in 54 Henry III [28 September 1270], found that Alan held Nort Multon in Devon of the king in chief by service of 1 serjeanty; Esseby la Zuche manor in Leicester of the heir of the earl of Winchester by service of 1 knight's fee; and in Sussex 2 1/2 knight's fees of Sir Henry de Percy and 1/2 fee of Sir Robert de Muhaut, and Alan's son Roger, aged 28 or 30, was his heir.<ref name="CalInqPMH3Vol1p234" />
Alan gave lands at Sibford to the Knights Templars.<ref name="dnbStanley414" /> He was also a benefactor of the Belmeis family foundation of Buildwas.<ref name="dnbStanley414" />
