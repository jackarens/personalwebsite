== Biography ==
David Erskine (9th Earl of Buchan) was born in 1672. He was the son of Henry Erskine (3rd Lord Cardross) and his wife Catherine Stewart.
He succeeded to the title of 4th Lord Cardross on 21 May 1693. He succeeded to the title of 9th Earl of Buchan in 1695. He succeeded to the title of 9th Lord Auchterhouse in 1695.
He died on 14 October 1745 in London, England. He was buried on 17 October 1745 at Hampstead, London, England. He died intestate and his estate was administered to a creditor on 12 December 1745.

===Career===
He was invested as a Privy Counsellor (P.C.) in 1697.2 He held the office of Governor of Blackness Castle between 1702 and 1707. He held the office of Governor of Blackness Castle between 1710 and 1714. He held the office of Lord-Lieutenant of Selkirkshire and Clackmannanshire between 1715 and 1734. He held the office of Representative Peer [Scotland] between 1715 and 1734. Due to his opposition to the Union, he was removed from all of his offices.

===Marriage===
* 1. On 11 February 1697 David Erskine was married to his first wife Frances Fairfax, daughter of Henry Fairfax and his wife Anne Browne.
* 2. On 15 September 1743 David Erskine was married tohis second wife Isabella Blackett, daughter of Sir William Blackett (1st Baronet)t. and his wife Julia Conyers.
