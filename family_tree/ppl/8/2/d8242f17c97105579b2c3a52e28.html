== Biography ==
Mary has been described by several names including Mariota, Mairead, Margaret and of course Mary. She is the daughter of [[Leslie-464|Walter Leslie]] Lord of Ross and [[Ross-1119|Euphemia Ross]] Countess of Ross. She was born the older of two children and likely shortly before 1365, her parents being married about 1363 and her father on Crusade in 1365.
She is alluded to in the Parliamentary Record of Scotland transcription of which, held in Perth on 24 October 1370 is readily available.<ref>Source: K.M. Brown et al eds (St Andrews, 2007-2013), 1370/10/1 </ref><ref>See also Antiquities of Aberdeen and Banff; vol 2, page 386. </ref> The transcription is held as a [[Space:Records_of_Parliament|Profile page]] for easy reference.
The Charter makes it clear that the Earldom of Ross would pass to Walter Leslie and Euphemia and be retained by the longest living. Thus Walter Leslie would hold the title in his own right and not simply in right of his wife.
The charter then states that legitimate heirs to Walter and Euphemia will inherit. It adds the clause that if there are no male heirs, and “perchance she has more daughters” then the eldest daughter “always” shall inherit without division. If there are no heirs then the title would revert to Joanna, the younger sister to Euphemia.
It is clear that, at this point (1370), Walter and Euphemia already had a daughter, Mary (Mariota) and, if we believe the History of Macdonalds and Lords of the Isles,<ref>See: History of Macdonalds and Lords of the Isles, page 61 </ref> already engaged to Donald, Lord of the Isles. One can only suppose this was part of the negotiations, between the High Steward, John, Lord of the Isles (Donald's father), and William, Earl of Ross. It could be presumed that Walter Leslie was not a willing partner to this arrangement or may not even have been there when it was made. There is mention<ref>[[#S-1]] MacLeod; page 57</ref> that she was married about 1380.
The son of Walter and Euphemia, [[Leslie-644|Alexander]], must have been born after this Parliament, but likely just before William, Earl of Ross, lodged his complaint to Robert Stewart, then in his capacity as King Robert II. Alexander is described as heir-apparent when the title is again granted in 1394 suggesting he was too young, but has inherited before 1398. Thus suggesting he was born about 1374.
[[Leslie-644|Alexander Leslie]] inherits on the death of his parents. He has a daughter Euphemia who, under the terms of the Charter, inherits the titles and lands as the Countess of Ross, in 1405. She was young when her father died and was held in ward. She was also known to be incapacitated or a hunchback. certainly her incapacity was used by Donald MacDonald, Lord of the Isles, as the excuse to claim the Earldom of Ross in right of his wife. This action was to lead to an invasion of Scotland and failure at the Battle of Harlaw. Donald, forced to return to his lands gave up the right to the title at Lochgilphead.
Mary or Mariota (Margaret, in the Historical Record of the Leslies) was however the eldest daughter of Sir Walter Leslie and Euphemia and, under the Charter of 1370, important should the line of Euphemia fail ��� which it did. Although there were attempts to marry her in 1413 and 1414, to relatives of the Regent, none were successful and she was advised to resign her titles and become a nun; which she did in 1415. The lands were provided to [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Stewart,_Earl_of_Buchan John Stewart, Earl of Buchan] who died without legitimate heirs. When Monarchy was restored in 1424 in the return of James I, the Albany Stewart's power was destroyed and the inheritance restored.
Mariota pressed her claim to the titles on the basis of the charter of 1370 and was allowed. She is not known to have called herself Countess of Ross although her son, Alexander, became Earl of Ross in 1437.

She is said to have died after her son gained the title and in 1440.
