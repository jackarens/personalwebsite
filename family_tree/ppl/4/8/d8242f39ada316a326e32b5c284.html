{{Great Britain|Scotland|Midlothian|Edinburgh}}
== Biography ==Mary Hope was the daughter of [[Hope-144|Sir Thomas Hope of Craighall, Bart, Lord Advocate]].<ref name=THDp84>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 84-5</ref>
Mary's parents had fourteen children,<ref name=JPBp99>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 99-100</ref> although it appears only six of them survived until adulthood.
On 24 April, 1635, Sir Thomas sent funds by the Earl of Mar to his son Mr Alexander, whereof &pound;90 sterling was for "jewellis" for Mary and the other &pound;30 was for him.<ref name=THDp24>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 24</ref> Was Mary with Alexander, who was with the Court of King Charles I, or was he just purchasing apparel for her?
On 23 April 1636, Mary's father paid 2600 merks to Patrick Wod for letters of exchange drawn on by his son Mr Alexander: 1100 for Alexander and the rest for gowns to Mary.<ref name=THDp42>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 42</ref> Was Mary still with Alexander at Court?
'''Mary writes to her brothers, Alexander and James''' Mary's father sent letters from her to her brothers, Alexander and James, on 14 January, 1637.<ref name=THDp53>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 53</ref> Alexander was at Court and James was at France.<ref name=THDp39>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 39</ref>
'''Mary Marries''' On 10 January, 1639, Sir Thomas recorded in his diary that his daughter Mary was contracted with Charles Erfkin. Thomas noted beside this entry, "Mary contractit."<ref name=THDp84 /> Sir Charles Erskine was the son of "Dame Marie Steuart, Countes of Mar".<ref name=THDp205>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 205</ref> Just under a month later, his daughter "Marie" and Charles Erfkein were married on Tuesday 5 February at Gray Freir Kirk, by Mr Andro Ramfay, in the presence of the "Countefs of Mar; the Lord Auchterhous; Alexander, John, and Mr William Erfkinis; myfelf, and my thrie fones, John, Thomas, and James; John Carftairs, Mr John Rollok, &c." Thomas noted beside this diary entry, "Mary maryit."<ref name=THDp84 /> In ''The Diary of Sir James Hope'', Balfour Paul notes that Mary's husband was generally known as Sir Charles Erskine of Cambuskenneth,<ref name=JPBp131>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 131.</ref> Mary's father refers to her in his diary as Lady Cambufkynneth,<ref name=THDp135>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 135</ref> and L. Cambufkynneth<ref name=THDp191>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 191</ref> and her brother James refers to her as Cambuskenneth.<ref name=JPBp131 />
Mary went to Craighall on 23 April 1639, and her father gave her for his "barne Thomas, the cognifance of Baronet, quhilk I gatt from Lord Stirling, 19 Merche 1630; and Anne [her sister?] went with hir."<ref name=THDp93>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 93</ref>
'''Mary's daughter is born''' On 15 November 1639, Marie was delivered of a maid child.<ref name=THDp111>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 111</ref>
'''Mary's son is born''' On 1 May 1641, Lady Cambufkynneth was delivered of a man child. Mary's father gave Jean Cuningham, the midwife, one double angel.<ref name=THDp135 />
'''Mary's daughter Marie dies''' On Friday, 17 June 1642, Mary's father wrote, "about 9 hours in the morning, my deir barne Marie Erfkin, (having lyen from 2 Junij in the pockis,) departed at 2 zieris and half-zeir aige." Mary was buried the following day in the Gray Frier yard.<ref name=THDp170>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 170-2</ref>
'''Mary's son Thomas dies''' A few weeks later, Thomas recorded another loss in the family on 2 July 1642, "My deir oy Thomas Erfkin departit this lyff ..." having taken sic on the Thursday after his sweet sister Marie died ... "The Lord gif me grace to be patient and obedient to his holy will." Thomas Erskine was buried beside his sister Mary in the Grey Freirs, with their grandparents.<ref name=THDp170 />
'''Mary's son John is born and dies''' The day after the burial of Mary's son, Thomas, between 3 and 4 in the afternoon on 4 July, Mary was safely delivered of one man child, baptised John by Mr Andro Ramfay on 5 July 1642, in memory of the Earl of Mar, his grandfather.<ref name=THDp170 /> On 26 September 1643, Thomas wrote "My oy John Erskin departit", his sweet babe John Erfkin was sick of a "defluction, and thair wes a cauter putt in his neck, be advyfe off Doctor Arnott; but he peynit an peynit ftill to the 5 of October 1643, ... at 6 in the morning; at quhilk tyme he departit." While the "barne" was sick, he was "erneft in prayer with my Lord" and sleeping while his wife helped attend the "barn".<ref name=THDp196>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 196-7</ref>
'''Mary's son Charles is born''' On 12 June 1643, Mary's husband went to Leith to start his journey to France, with the Earl of Hadingtoun and William Erfkine, his brother german.<ref name=THDp189>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 189</ref> Mary was heavily pregnant when he left because several weeks later, on 1 July, Sir Thomas writes, "My dauchter L. Cambufkynneth delyverit of a man chyld about 7 hours in the morning, ... for quhilk I bliffe the Lord." The "barne baptiffit" on 4 July, 1643, by Mr William Colvin; witnessed by the Earl of Buchan, Scottiscraig, Mary's four brothers, and her father. Sir Thomas "held up the babe and namit him Charles, from his father being abfent in France."<ref name=THDp191 />
'''Mary's brother Thomas dies''' Mary's father wrote in his diary, "This 19 Auguft 1643 the faittis of feikness increafcit on my deir fone Sir Thomas, quhilk he tuik firft on Thurfday the 10 Auguft ... My deir fone departit on 23 Auguft 1643 ... The Lord comfort me and his poore bedfellow, and fupplie hir and my wants, and bliff his deir children."<ref name=THDp194>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 194</ref>
'''Mary's mother-in-law dies''' Sir Charles came home from France on the "fevint" of May 1644. His mother, Mary's mother-in-law, "Dame Marie Steuart, Countes of Mar", took a "deidlie brafche" on 1 May, 1644, dying ten days later at the house of Mary's father in the Cowgait [at Leith?]. Her Ladyship's funeral was to be on Thursday, 23 May, at Alloway.<ref name=THDp205 />
On 7 March, 1645, Sir Thomas wrote to his son[-in-law] Sir Charles, including letters from him to his daughter, Sir Charles' spouse; the "babie Sir James"; and one to Sir Charles from the Earl of Mar. On 17 March her father received a letter from his "dauchter Marie."<ref name=THDp214>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 214-5</ref>
'''Mary 's son James is born''' About two hours after midnight on 21 March, 1645, Mary was safely delivered of a man child. Mary's father Thomas wrote about this news: "Bleffit be the Lord, quho laidis us daylie with benefits."<ref name=THDp214 /> James Erskine's baptism on 25 March was witnessed by James Earl Buchan, James Hamiltoun, brother to the Earl of Hadingtoun, Mary's brother, Sir James, and James Steuart, Mr Mungo Law, minifter.<ref name=THDp214 />
'''Mary's sister Anne is to marry''' Mary's father sent the articles of the contract "anent my dauchter Anne" to Mary's husband, Sir Charles, on 25 March 1645.<ref name=THDp215>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 215</ref>
'''Mary's father dies''' Mary was not at her father's bedside during his last illness. Her brother James wrote in his diary on 27 September, 1646, "Then he gave blessing to my sister Cambuskenneth and her houseband who was at London and her sone Charles, then to my sister Cardross and my Lord."<ref name=JPBp131 /> Sir Thomas died peaceably between 9 and 10 o'clock at night on 1 October, 1646.<ref name=JPBp132>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 132-3.</ref>
'''Mary's sister Anne dies''' Lady Cardross died on Wednesday, 20 May 1653.<ref name=JPBp159>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 159</ref>
'''Mary's brother John dies''' Sir John Hope of Craighall died on 27 April 1654, their brother Sir James says he died because "his spirits wer spent, an indispositione in his stomache, and generall dibilitie and defect of naturall heat throw his whole body.<ref name=JPBp168>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 168</ref>
