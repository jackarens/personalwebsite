== Biography ==
Somerled was the son of Gille-Brigde (Gillebride), son of Gille-Adomnain. Somerled is first mentioned in 1153, when he rebelled against [[Dunkeld-41|King Malcolm IV]] (d.1165). While no record exists for his birth it is highly probable he was born before 1130 and likely in the first or second decade of the twelfth century. He made peace with King Malcolm in 1160, but rebelled again in 1164, when, at Renfrew, he was killed in battle with his son, [[MacGillebride-9|Gille Brigte]], whom he fathered by an unknown woman. His wife,[[Olafsdatter-5|Ragnhild]], is a daughter of [[Godredsson-10|Olaf]] (d.1153), son of [[Haraldsson-4|Godred]], king of the Isles, and they had three sons, [[Macsomerled-3|Dugald]], [[Somerled-1|Ranald]], and [[Macsomerled-5|Angus]], and perhaps a fourth, [[Sumarlidasson-2|Olaf]]. He also had a daughter, [[Macsomerled-4|Bethoc]], who was the first prioress of Iona Nunnery.
From Somerled's son, [[Macsomerled-3|Dugald]], are descended the lords of Argyll and the clan MacDougall, and from his son, [[Somerled-1|Ranald]], the MacDonald lords of the Isles and also MacRuaris.
