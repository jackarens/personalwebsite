== Biography ==
Sheriff of Worcester: Baron of Elmley

William and his wife had eight children:
i) WILLIAM V de Beauchamp ([1237/41]-Elmley 5 or 9 Jun 1298, bur 22 Jun 1298 Worcester, Friars Minor).

ii) JOHN de Beauchamp of Holt (-after [1261/69])

iii) WALTER de Beauchamp of Alcester

iv) JOAN de Beauchamp (-after 1298)

v) ISABEL de Beauchamp

vi) MARGARET de Beauchamp (-after 1284)

vii) SIBYLLA de Beauchamp

viii) SARAH de Beauchamp (-after Jul 1317)
On 11 June 1250, Henry III appointed proctors to argue the king's jurisdiction in a cause in the pope's court between William de Bello Campo, Sheriff of Worcester, and certain of the king's bailiffs, on the one hand, and the bishop of Worcester on the other. <ref name="PatentsHenIIIp65">{{EE citation|editor1-last= Assistant Keeper of HM Public Records|title=''Calendar of the Patent Rolls Preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=London|publisher=His Majesty's Stationery Office|volume=Henry III 1247-1258|year=1908|accessdate=10 October 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/patentrollsreig00unkngoog#page/n76/mode/2up|pages=65}}</ref>
