'''Guillaume de Valence, Lord of Pembroke and Wexford'''<ref name="WAdeValence">The Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster, "William and Aymer de Valence", ''Westminster Abbey'', 2014, http://westminster-abbey.org/our-history/people/william-and-aymer-de-valence, Accessed 14 August, 2014</ref>
Guillaume [Eng: William] de Valence was the son of [[Lusignan-2|Hugh de Lusignan, Comte de la Marche]], and [[Angoulême-40|Isabella of Angouleme]], the widow of King John of England.<ref name="WAdeValence" />
Guillaume was probably born in Valence near Lusignan in France.<ref name="WAdeValence" /> He may have been born in 1225 and baptised at the the Cistercian Abbey in Valence.

Guillaume was the brother of:
#[[Lusignan-48|Agatha (Agnes) de Lusignan]]
#[[Lusignan-24|Marguerite de Lusignan]]
#[[Lusignan-23|Geoffrey de Lusignan]]
#[[Lusignan-25|Isabella de Lusignan]]
#[[Lusignan-3|Hugh de Lusignan]]
#[[Lusignan-22|Guy de Lusignan]];<ref name="WAdeValence" />
#[[Lusignan-1|Alice de Lusignan]];<ref name="WAdeValence" />
#[[Lusignan-26|Aymer (Ademar) de Lusignan]];<ref name="WAdeValence" />
He was also the half-brother of Henry III, King of England.<ref name="WAdeValence" /> At Henry's invitation, William, Guy, Aymer and Alice went to England in 1247.<ref name="WAdeValence" />
Guillaume married [[Munchensy-17|Joan Munchensy]], the daughter of Warin de Munchensy, Lord of Swanscombe on 13 August 1247.<ref name="WAdeValence" /> Through this marriage he acquired vast lands in England and Ireland.<ref name="WAdeValence" />

Guillaume and Joan had children:#[[Valence-16|Agnes (Ann) de Valence]], who married three times;<ref name="WAdeValence" />#[[Valence-2|Isabel de Valence]] who married John Hastings;<ref name="WAdeValence" />#[[Valence-10|Joan (Johanna) de Valence]], who married John Comyn;<ref name="WAdeValence" />
#Margaret, died 1276;<ref name="WAdeValence" />
#John, died 1477.<ref name="WAdeValence" />#[[Valence-13|William de Valence]], died 1282;<ref name="WAdeValence" /> and#[[Valence-14|Aymer de Valence]], born about 1270, died 23 June 1324, married 1) Beatrice, died 1320, the daughter of Raoul de Clermont, and 2) Mary de St Pol, the daughter of Guy de Chatillon, Count of St Pol, on 5 July 1321;<ref name="WAdeValence" />
Guillaume was never created Earl of Pembroke.<ref name="WAdeValence" /> He went on Crusade with Prince Edward.<ref name="WAdeValence" />
Guillaume de Valence, Lord of Pembroke and Wexford died on 16 May 1296, at his manor in Kent, England, and his stone and brass tomb is in the Chapel of St Edmund and St. Thomas the Martyr in Westminster Abbey.<ref name="WAdeValence" /> "His tomb is the only existing example in England of Limoges champlevé enamel-work."<ref name="WAdeValence" /> Translated, the inscription on his tomb, which was recorded by Wlliam Camden in 1600, says:
:"''All England, thou weepest, for the royal progeny with which thou wert accustomed to bloom is dead. William, whom the humblest tomb contains, shows forth an illustrious name, Valence, a noble surname, such as he ought to claim for himself. Valiant, he prevailed, victorious by virtue and valour; and, peaceful, gave pleasure with his vigour of mind and of character; generous, capable, steadfast; eagerly prosecuting wars, competent and modest, faithful, striving for distinction. In 1300 less four years in the month of May death, with his own sword, struck him down. Thou who readest these words remember how full of fear is the way, and see how both thou and I shall die, all unknowing of the hour. O merciful Christ, let him, I pray, enter Heaven, and let him see nothing melancholy; for he excelled in all these ways.”''<ref name="WAdeValence" />
Guillaume's children Margaret, John and Aymer were also buried in Westminster Abbey.<ref name="WAdeValence" />
