== Biography ==
Sir John was the natural son of [[Stewart-1686|King Robert III]] <ref name=Burke>Bernard Burke, Royal Descents and Pedigrees of Founders' Kin, Pedigree VIII (1864), https://books.google.com/books?id=SxYYAAAAYAAJ&dq=John%20stewart%20of%20Blackhall&pg=PA114#v=onepage</ref><ref name=Crawfurd>George Crawfurd, A General Description of the Shire of Renfrew (1818), p. 58, https://books.google.com/books?id=bpnmAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA58</ref><ref name=Colburn>Henry Colburn, A Genealogical and Heraldic Dictionary of the Peerage and Baronetage of the British Empire, Volume 42 (1880), Part 1, p. cix, https://books.google.com/books?id=u6IaAAAAYAAJ&pg=PR109 </ref><ref name=Thomson>Oliver Thomson, The Rises and Falls of the Royal Stewarts (2011), p. 73, 214, https://books.google.com/books?id=WTg7AwAAQBAJ&pg=PA206</ref><ref name=Cawley>Charles Cawley, Medieval Lands, A prosopography of medieval European noble and royal families, http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/SCOTLAND.htm#_ftnref1263</ref><ref name=wiki1>"Robert III of Scotland", Wikipedia, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_III_of_Scotland#Family_and_issue, Note: His father's page, This John does not have has own article </ref> and an unknown mistress, (possibly by a daughter of Sir Archibald Campbell of Lochow).<ref name=wiki2> "Barony of Blackhall", Wikipedia, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barony_of_Blackhall</ref> His birth date is suggested to be around or as early as 1355 in some derivative sources. <ref name=Olar>Jared Olar, "The Blackhall Connection", The Stewarts of Balquhidder Research Group, http://www.chuckspeed.com/balquhidder/history/Blackhall.htm, accessed Mar 1, 2015 </ref> A date of death around 1412 or 1413 at Blackhall has been cited by some sources. <ref name=Olar/><ref name=wiki2/>
He is an ancestor of the Shaw-Stewarts of Ardgowan and Blackhall. <ref name=wiki1/> This John is also the ancestor of many other Stewarts including Kildonan, Castle Levin, and Newark.<ref name=Thomson/> Auchingowan, Carnock, Christwell, Gask, Greenock, Kincarachy, Kirkwood, Lumloch, Newton, Pardovan, Scotstoun, Steuarthall and Wester Polmaise

=== Timeline === * 1390 - John received charter for the lands of Auchingowan (Auchingoun) in Renfrewshire. <ref name=Burke/><ref name=Crawfurd/><ref name=Cawley/>* 1395 (Dec 12) - John received by royal charter signed by Dundonald for the lands of Blackhall in Renfrewshire and Lanarkshire. <ref name=wiki1/><ref name=Burke/><ref name=Crawfurd/><ref name=Colburn/><ref name=Cawley/>* 1403 - John received charter for the lands of Ardgowan in Renfrewshire. <ref name=wiki1/><ref name=Burke/><ref name=Crawfurd/><ref name=Colburn/><ref name=Cawley/> He also rebuilt the castle at Ardowan. <ref name=Thomson/>

=== Family ===
John's wife is unknown. He had two known children:* [[Stewart-2338|Margaret]], who was the second wife of Sir Duncan Campbell of Lochow, son of Sir Colin Campbell of Lochow, Argyllshire & his wife Mariot Campbell.<ref name=Cawley/>* John, who inherited the Barony of Blackhall from his father in 1414. <ref name=wiki2/> He married Elizabeth Lyle, daughter of Sir Robert Lyle, who later became 1st Lord Lyle<ref name=Burke/> He passed away about 1442. <ref name=wiki2/>

== Similar John Stewarts ==There are at least two notable John Stewarts in Scotland in the latter half of the 14th century to the early part of the 15th century. One was a son of [[Stewart-1532|Alexander Stewart]] of Darnley who is documented as wikitree profile [[Stewart-2351]]. John Stewart, the natural (illegitimate) son of [[Stewart-972|Robert III, King of the Scots]] is the subject of this wikitree profile.
