== Biography ==
We know little about the first Sir Thomas Boyd,who succeeded to the Boyd,estates.He evidently followed the Family's war-like traits,as he accompanied King DavidII to Durham and was captured by the English at thebattle of Neville's Cross in 1346.Either he or his son built Dean Castle,replacing the wooden fort atop the motte.Dean Castle was to be center of
the Kilmarnock Estates until the 1700's.

Sir Thomas (The Archer) 2nd Feudal Baron of Kilmarnock Boyd b. c1323 From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia: He was a 14th century Scottish landowner and lord of Kilmarnock in Ayrshire. He was the eldest son and heir of Robert Boyd, (who fought at the Battle of Bannockburn 24 June 1314). â€ƒSir Thomas the Archer Boyd had from King David I1 a grant of the forfeiture of William Carpenter and he accompanied that monarch to the Battle of Durnam (or Neville's Cross), 0ctbber 17, 1346, where he was taken prisoner with his Royal master, taken to London, and imprisoned for a time. (This battle was the culmination of a Scottish invasion of northern England. The battle ended with the rout of the Scots and the capture of their king, David II of Scotland) In October, 1367, a treaty was made for David's ransom, and no doubt Sir Thomas was ransomed at or-before that time. Sir Thomas the Archer Boyd is known for building the earliest parts of Dean Castle around 1350, He had three sons and was succeeded by his son, Thomas (died 1432). Sir Thomas' later descendants were the Earls of Kilmarnock. [1] .His sons were:
Sir Thomas: 3rd Feudal Baron of Kilmarnock.
William: ancestor of the Boyds of Badenheath.
Robert de Boyd: ancestor of the Boyds of Portincross.
