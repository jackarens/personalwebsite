== Biography ==
===Family Matters===Thomas Hope of Craighall was born circa 1573.<ref name="Hopetoun">"A History of the Hope Family", Hopetoun House Preservation Trust, http://www.hopetoun.co.uk/History-of-the-Hope-Family.html, accessed 15 February 2015.</ref>
Thomas was the son of Henry Hope, burgess of Dieppe and Edinburgh,<ref name="Hopetoun" /> and his wife, Jaqueline de Tott.<ref name="dnb25TH1646">{{EE citation|title=''Dictionary of National Biography''|editor1-last=Lee|editor1-first=Sidney|year=1891|publication-place=London|publisher=Smith, Elder, & co. |volume=Vol. XXVII Hindmarsh-Hovenden|dateaccessed=17 February 2015|repository=Archive.org|url=https://archive.org/stream/dictionarynatio25stepgoog#page/n346/mode/2up|pages=326-7}}</ref>
Thomas had a younger brother Henry, who settled in Amsterdam and was the ancestor of the rich family of merchants connected with that city.<ref name="dnb25TH1646" /> Or an older brother, Henry, eldest son of Henry, who also had a son Henry or Harie,<ref name=JPBp131>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 131</ref> and a son James the ancestor of the Hopes of Amsterdam;<ref name=JPBp132>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 132</ref>
Thomas married Elizabeth, the daughter of John Binning or Bennet of Wallyford, co Haddington,<ref name="dnb25TH1646" /> in or before 1602.<ref name=JPBp131 />
Sir Thomas and Elizabeth had fourteen<ref name=JPBp99>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 99-100</ref> children:#John, lord Craighall, born 1605?<ref name="dnb25TH1646" /> died 27 April 1654,<ref name=JPBp168>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 168</ref> on Cromwell's Committee of Justice,<ref name=JPBp151>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 151</ref> married secondly, Rachell, widow of Sir John Skene of Curriehill;<ref name=JPBp158>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 158</ref> #Sir Thomas Hope (1606-1643) of Kerse,<ref name="dnb25TH1646" /> and Wester Granton, second son, married, before 30 December 1630, Helen Rae, the third daughter and coheir of Adam Rae of Pitsindie;<ref name=JPBp131 /> Helen died 20 June 1647<ref name=JPBp138>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 138-9</ref>#Sir<ref name=JPBp131 /> Alexander, a cupbearer to Charles I;<ref name="dnb25TH1646" />#[[Hope-510|Sir James Hope of Hopetoun]] (4 July 1614<ref name=JPBp99 />-1661), sixth son, who married 1) on 11 January 1639 at Greyfriars Church, to [[Foulis-19|Ann Foulis]], died 1656, the daughter and heir of Sara Speir and her husband, Robert Foulis, advocate, son of James Foulis of Colinton, and 2) on 29 October 1657, Mary, the daughter and coheir of William Keith, seventh Earl of Marischal;<ref name=JPBp104>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 104-5</ref>#Mary, baptised 13 June 1620, referred to by her brother James as "my sister Cambuskenneth",<ref name=JPBp131 /> who was married to Sir Charles Erskine of Alva,<ref name="dnb25TH1646" /> or Charles Erskine of Bandeath, on 5 February, 1639. Charles, generally known as Sir Charles Erskine of Cambuskenneth, was the son of John, Earl of Mar.<ref name=JPBp131 /> Mary and Charles had children:##Charles, third son, born 1643, died 4 June 1690, and married about 1670 Christian, the daughter of Sir James Dundas of Arniston;<ref name=JPBp131 /> and #Anne, baptised 9 April, 1625, youngest daughter, married in 1645, David Erskine, Lord Cardross, eighth Earl of Mar, who succeeded his grandfather, John, seventh Earl of Mar.<ref name=JPBp131 /> <ref name=JPBp150>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 150</ref> Lady Cardross died on 20 May 1653.<ref name=JPBp159>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 159</ref>
Thomas provided for his children. On 29 September 1627, he prevailed upon the Earl of Annandale to fulfill the Earl's previous offer of giving Thomas contentment through finding Thomas' son, Alexander, a position with his sacred Majestie. On 24 February, 1636, he sent James to the university of Orleans in France and Alexander to Court. Thomas recalled James before 5 July, 1637, and paid the debts James had acquired.<ref name=JPBp99 /> His daughters married well, also: Mary's husband, Charles, was a younger son of John, Earl of Mar; and Anne's husband, David, was the Earl's grandson and heir.<ref name=JPBp99 /> This means Charles was David's uncle.
He bought James a new suit in December 1637. Balfour Paul suggests it was to be his wedding suit because James was married to Anna Foulis in Greyfriars Church on Thursday 11 January 1639. Balfour Paul also relays a "doubtful" story of Anna seeking the Lord Advocate's advice because her uncle and guardian, Sir William Baillie of Lamington was attempting to defraud her, and "the matter ended by her marrying the Lord Advocate's son."<ref name=JPBp101>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 101</ref>

===Community Involvement===Sir Thomas Hope of Craighall studied law and was in 1626 appointed King's Advocate by Charles I,<ref name="Hopetoun" /> from 29 May jointly with William Oliphant.<ref name="dnb25TH1646" />
In February 1628, Thomas was rewarded for his services to the crown with a Nova Scotia baronetcy.<ref name="dnb25TH1646" />

===Death and Burial===Sir James Hope, Thomas' son, wrote about his father's death in his diary:<ref name=JPBp129>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 129-133</ref> :On 26 September, 1646, Thomas complained of backache which "tormented him sore". The following day his family left him sleeping while they went to Sunday church and found on their return he had fallen into a "Lethargie", could not move, and had difficulty speaking. When summoned, Doctors Kinked [Alexander Kincaid, Edinburgh doctor of medicine] and Purvice [Henry Purves, Edinburgh surgeon], gave him a "cleister" and applied "ventoses" ie an injection ''per rectum'' and cupping glasses. Over the next three days Thomas called for his family to give them his blessing and discuss his last wishes, spoke to his servitors, Mr George Norvell, Martin Crawfurd and John Mcgachen, dictated some letters with acuity. On 30 September, his pain returned, and the following day he sang the 23rd psalm after which he spoke little.
:Thomas died peaceably between 9 and 10 o'clock at night on 1 October, 1646.
:On Tuesday 6 October, Sir Thomas Hope of Craighall, first Baronet, was buried in Grayfriers "besyde ye wester dyke yreof betuixt it and ye stonne yt stands at D M M [Dame Margaret Mary] ye Lady Craighalls head. My brother Kerse lyes close by his right hand..."
Thomas left provision for his wife of "ye house in Edr and to Grantoune and ye house yreof, and the maines of Craighall and ye house yreof; bot shee is ordeined to quitte one ... shee keepes ye summe of 2500mks by yis."<ref name=JPBp133>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], pp. 133</ref>
Only four of Thomas' sons survived childhood, and of these three became judges: Sir John of Craighall, Sir Thomas of Kerse and Sir James of Hopetoun.<ref name=JPBp104 />
