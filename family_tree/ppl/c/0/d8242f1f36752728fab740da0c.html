== Biography ==James II is the son of [[Stewart-419|James I]] and [[Beaufort-20|Joan Beaufort]]. He succeeded to the throne in 1437, at the age of six, when his father was murdered. Prior to becoming king he held the title [http://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Category:Duke_of_Rothesay Duke of Rothesay].
In 1449, he married Mary of Guelders, daughter of the Duke of Gelderland and together they had seven children. James had another child, John Stewart, Lord of Sticks, by an unknown mistress.
James was killed at a seige of Roxburgh Castle, August 1460, when a cannon he lit exploded. He was succeeded by his son, also named James, who was only eight at the time.
