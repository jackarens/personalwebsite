== Biography ==Alice, daughter of William de Warenne, Earl of Surrey and Sussex, and Joan de Vere.<ref>Cokayne, [https://archive.org/stream/completepeerageo01coka#page/241/mode/1up The complete peerage of England, Scotland, Ireland, Great Britain and the United Kingdom], p.241-241, added 2014-05-21, [[Bairfield-1|amb]]</ref>
Married Edmund Fitzalan, 9th Earl of Arundel, son of Richard Fitzalan, 8th Earl of Arundel and Alice of Saluzzo by 1305. <ref>Cokayne, p. 242</ref>
They had four sons (Sir Richard, Earl of Arundel & Surrey; John; Edmund, Treasurer of Chichester; & Michael, clerk) & 6 daughters (Eleanor, wife of William de St. John, & ofSir Gerard, 1st Lord Lisle; Alice, wife of Sir John de Bohun, Earl of Hereford & Essex; Katherine, wife of Sir Henry, 2nd Lord Husee, & of Sir Andrew Peverell; Aline, wife of Sir Roger, 5th Lord le Strange of Knockin; Elizabeth, wife of Sir William, 4th Lord Latimer; & Mary, wife of Sir John, 4th Lord le Strange of Blackmere. <ref>Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. I, p. 209-210</ref><ref>Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. II, p. 178-179.</ref>
The Earl of Arundel was captured in Shropshire by the Queen's party. On 17 November 1326 in Hereford, Arundel was beheaded by order of the Queen, leaving Alice de Warenne a widow. Her husband's estates and titles were forfeited to the Crown following Arundel's execution, but later restored to her eldest son, Richard.
