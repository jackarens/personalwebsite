== Biography ==
John Stewart, 1st Earl of Lennox (b. bef. 1430, d. 8 Jul/11 Sep 1495) was known as Lord Darnley and later as the Earl of Lennox. John was the son of [[Stewart-2350|Sir Alan Stewart]] and [[Seton-30|Catherine Seton]] and has been confused with another [[Stewart-1555|John Stewart]], the son of [[Stewart-4030|James Stewart]] "The Black Knight of Lorn" and [[Beaufort-20|Joan Beaufort]], widow of [[Stewart-419|King James I]]. Joan's husbands also being confused in the past.

== Biography ==
John Stuart (1st Earl of Lennox) was born before c. 1422. He was the son of Sir Alan Stuart of Darnley and his wife Catherine Seton.

He died between 8 July 1495 and 11 September 1495.

===Career===
John Stewart was created 1st Lord of Darnley circa 1460. He was created 1st Earl of Lennox on 6 August 1473. He held the office of Lord Warden of the West March in 1481.In 1482 he was one of the lords who seized King James III, but was pardoned. He held the office of Keeper of Dumbarton Castle in 1488.

===Marriage===
On 15 May 1438 John Stewart married his first wife Margaret Montgomerie, daughter of Alexander Montgomerie (1st Lord Montgomerie) and his wife Margaret Boyd by indenture, with both parties being underage.

===Children===

* 7. Sir Robert Stuart, 4th Seigneur d'Aubigny. b. c 1470, d. 1543.
