== Biography ==
Sir John Maxwell was Lord of Caerlaverock and second son of [[Maxwell-171|John Maxwell]].<ref name=Fraser>Sir William Fraser, ''The Book of Carlaverock'' (Edinburgh: unknown, 1873), p. 106-109, digital images, https://archive.org/stream/bookofcarlaverv100fras#page/106/mode/1up. Internet Archive (https://archive.org : accessed 29 October 2016).</ref>

He had the following children:
* [[Maxwell-990|Herbert]]<ref name=Fraser/>
* [[Maxwell-169|John]]<ref name=Fraser/>* [[Maxwell-638|Agnes]], married Sir Robert Pollok of that Ilk in the County of Renfrew.<ref name=Fraser/><ref name=Burke>Sir Bernard Burke, ''A Genealogical and Heraldic Dictionary of the Peerage and Baronetage of the British Empire, 30th Edition'' (London: Harrison, 1868), p. 887, digital images, https://books.google.com/books?id=K2FHAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA887 (http://books.google.com : accessed 31 July 2016).</ref><ref name=Metcalfe> William Musham Metcalfe, ''A History of the County of Renfrew from the Earliest Times'' (Paisley: Alexander Gardner, 1905), p. 95, digital images, https://books.google.com/books?id=OC8DAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA95 (http://books.google.com : accessed 31 July 2016).</ref>
It was this John Maxwell who was the grandfather of John De Pollok, the son of Robert De Pollok and Agnes Maxwell, who gave the said John De Pollok a grant of land in Scotland per charter dated 3 May 1372<ref>"History of the Shire of Renfrew," pg,. 290</ref>. The marriage of Robert De Pollok and Agnes Maxwell is the earliest connection known between the Maxwells and the Polloks of Renfrewshire.
