=== Biography ===:Sir James Douglas of Dalkeith was born about 1338 to Sir John Douglas and Agnes Monfode, her parents are unknown. <ref>John Douglas DOB is projected from the marriage date of his parents</ref> Sir James Douglas died about 1420. <ref>[http://thepeerage.com/p10793.htm#i107929 Sir James Douglas of Dalkeith1 M, #107929, d. 1420]</ref>
:Sir James Douglas of Dalkeith succeeded his uncle, Sir William of Liddesdale, and was well known for his learning and accomplishments. Sir James was married in 1372 to Agnes Dunbar, daughter of Sir Patrick Dunbar. <ref>[http://thepeerage.com/p39238.htm#i392380 Lady Agnes Dunbar: The Peerage]</ref> They had four children, including a son, before her death in 1378. Sir James' second wife was Egida, half-sister of King Robert II. Sir James died in 1420. <ref>Sir James Douglas of Dalkeith: The Douglas Archives</ref> <ref> Maxwell, Herbert. A History of the House of Douglas from the Earliest Times Down to the Legislative Union of England and Scotland. London: Freemantle, 1902. (p. 236-237)</ref>
:Note: Sir James Douglas title is 1st Laird of Dalkeith, or of Dalkeith. Although Sir James sat as a member of Parlement, the title was not as yet hereditary. His son Sir James Douglas, is correctly styled !st Lord Dalkeith.
