== In Brief ==:Maud la Zouche was born about 1290 to Alan la Zouche and his wife Eleanor Segrave.<ref name=MCA>Douglas Richardson, ''[http://www.royalancestry.net/ Magna Carta Ancestry: A Study in Colonial and Medieval Families]'', Royal Ancestry series, 2nd edition, 4 vols., ed. Kimball G. Everingham, (Salt Lake City, Utah: the author, 2011), Vol 2, pp 392-5</ref> She was born in in Ashby-de-la-Zouche, Leicestershire, England.{{Citation needed}}
:Maud married Robert de Holand, Knt., "before 1309/10 (being contracted to marry in or before 1305/6)". He was born about 1283, son and heir of Robert de Holand, Knt., by Elizabeth, daughter and co-heiress of William de Samlesbury, Knt.<ref name=MCA />

== Biography ==: Maud, daughter and co-heiress of Alan la Zouch, married Robert de Holand, Knt., "of Upholland and ... SAMLESBURY, and ... LANCASHIRE, ... and, in right of his wife, of ... Leicestershire, ... NORTHAMPTONSHFIRE, ... Leicestershire, ... Warwickshire, etc. [ROBERT DE HOLAND] was Justice of Chester, Constable of Beeston Castle. ... He subsequently became chief councillor of Earl Thomas, and thus grew in importance and wealth."<ref name=RA>Douglas Richardson, ''[http://www.royalancestory.net Royal Ancestry: A Study in Colonial and Medieval Families],'' 5 vols., ed. Kimball G. Everingham, (Salt Lake City, Utah: the author, 2013), Vol. III, pages 292-299; see also Vol. IV p. 431</ref>
: Robert was "son and heir of [[Holland-760|ROBERT DE HOLAND]], Knt., of Upholland (in Wigan), Lancashire, by [[Samlesbury-7|ELIZABETH SAMLESBURY]], daug. and co-heiress of [[Samlesbury-6|WILLIAM DE SAMLESBURY]], Knt. of Samlesbury and Over Darwen, Lancashire."<ref name=RA />
: "In 1305-6 her [Maud's] great aunt, Emeline Longespee, widow of Maurice Fitz Maurice, Knt., settled the reversion of Denford ..., Berkshire on Maud, being then betrothed to marry Robert de Holand. In 1310 he [Robert de Holand] founded a college of priests in the Chapel of St. Thomas at Upholland, later altered to a Priory of Benedictine monks. ... He [Robert de Holand] took sides with Earl Thomas in various contentions with the king, being pardoned in 1313 for his complicity in the death of Sir Peter de Gavaston, Earl of Cornwall. He was summoned to Parliament from 29 July 1314 to 15 May 1321, ... whereby he is held to have become Lord Holand (Holland). He was heir in or bef. 1318 to his younger brother, [[Holland-623|WILLIAM DE HOLAND]] of Euxton, Lancashire. In 1322 he and his wife, Maud, settled the manors of Ridlington, Rutland, and Torrisholme, Lancashire, a moiety of the manors of Breightmet ... Lancashire on themselves for life, with successive remainders to their sons, Alan, Robert, and Thomas, and their respective heirs male. On the accession of King Edward III, Robert petitioned for the restitution of his lands, which petition was granted 23 Dec 1327. ... SIR ROBERT DE HOLAND 1st Lord Holand, was captured by some adherents of his former patron, Earl Thomas, in Boreham Wood, Elstree, Hertfordshire 7 Oct. 1328, who cut off his head for his treachery. ... In 1329 Mary widow of Aymer de Valence, Earl of Pembroke, had license to demise her interest in the Castle and manor of Thorpe Waterville, with the hamlets of Achurch and Aldwinkle, Northamptonshire, to MAUD widow of ROBERT DE HOLAND. In 1339 she (Maud) sued her son, [[Holland-597|ROBERT DE HOLAND]] in a plea that he should warrant to her half the manor of Bagworth, Leicestershire ... Robert did not appear, .... She [Maud] was a legatee in the 1347 will of John de Warenne, Earl of Surrey, who bequeathed her four carriage horses.<ref name=RA />
:''Complete Peerage'' writes:<ref>''Complete Peerage,'' VI:528-31, (transcribed by Dave Utzinger)</ref><blockquote>He [Robert de Holand] married, about 1311, certainly before August 1314, Maud, daughter and coheir of Alan (LA ZOUCHE), LORD ZOUCHE, with whom he acquired extensive estates, including the manor of Brackley, Northants. He died as aforesaid, 7 October 1328, and is said to have been buried in the Grey Friars' Church at Preston, Lancs, to which he had been a benefactor. At the Queen's request provision was made for the wife and children while his lands were in the King's hand. His widow, who was going on a pilgrimage to Santiago in 1336, died 31 May 1349, and was buried at Brackley.</blockquote>
:Burke writes:<ref>Sir Bernard Burke, Dormant, Abeyant, Forfeited, and Extinct Peerages, Burke's Peerage, Ltd., London, 1883, p. 599, Zouche, Baron Zouche, of Ashby, co. Leicester</ref><blockquote>Maud m. Robert, Lord Holland. Their great-granddau. and heir-general, Maud Holland, m. 1373, John Lovel, Lord Lovel, of Tichmersh.</blockquote>

== Descent from Magna Carta Surety Baron ==
:Maud la Zouche is descended from Magna Carta surety baron Saher Quincy, per Douglas Richardson's [http://books.google.co.id/books?id=8JcbV309c5UC&pg=RA1-PA394 Magna Carta Ancestry].<ref name=MCA /> She is the 4th great-granddaughter of Magna Charta Surety Saher de Quincy (through her father Alan la Zouche, whose grandmother [[Quincy-145|Ellen]] was [[Quincy-226|Saher de Quincy]]'s granddaughter).<ref>Douglas Richardson, ''[http://www.royalancestry.net/ Magna Carta Ancestry: A Study in Colonial and Medieval Families],'' Royal Ancestry series, 2nd edition, 4 vols., ed. Kimball G. Everingham, (Salt Lake City, Utah: the author, 2011), Vol. III, page 416 iii and [https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=8JcbV309c5UC&pg=PA412 Vol. I, page 412, #3 - page 416 #5]. </ref>
