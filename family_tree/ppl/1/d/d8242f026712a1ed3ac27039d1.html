== Biography =='''Sir Charles Erskine of Alva, Cambuskenneth and Bandeath, Laird Bandeath'''<ref name=RPS>''The Records of the Parliaments of Scotland to 1707'', K.M. Brown et al eds (St Andrews, 2007-2015), 1641/7/2. accessed: 9 March 2015. http://www.rps.ac.uk/trans/1641/7/2</ref>
Charles Erskine was christened on 5 November 1615 at Alloa, Clackmannan, Scotland.<ref name= "Bap">''Scotland, Births and Baptisms, 1564-1950,'' index, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/XY87-N93 : accessed 10 March 2015), Charles Erskine, 05 Nov 1615; citing ALLOA,CLACKMANNAN,SCOTLAND, reference ; FHL microfilm 1,040,205.</ref>
Charles was the son of John Erskine, Earl Mar, and Mary Stuart.<ref name= "Bap" />
Balfour Paul notes.<ref>[[#S-1]] Balfour Paul, Vol 5, page 622. </ref> that Charles married, firstly (contract 10 January 1639), Mary, third daughter of Sir Thomas Hope of Craighall; secondly, Helen, daughter of Sir James Skene of Curriehill, and widow of Sir Robert Bruce of Broomhall. She survived him and was married to her third husband in 1666, Sir James Dundas of Arniston. While this notice exists Balfour Paul does not provide the names of his children and their respective mothers.
'''Charles marries Mary Hope''' On 10 January, 1639, [[Hope-144|Sir Thomas Hope of Craighall, Bart]], Lord Advocate of Scotland, recorded in his diary that his daughter Mary was contracted with Charles Erfkin.<ref name=THDp84>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 84</ref> Sir Charles Erskine was the son of "Dame Marie Steuart, Countes of Mar".<ref name=THDp205>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 205</ref> Just under a month later, his daughter "Marie" and Charles Erfkein were married on Tuesday 5 February at Gray Freir Kirk, by Mr Andro Ramfay, in the presence of the "Countefs of Mar; the Lord Auchterhous; Alexander, John, and Mr William Erfkinis; myfelf, and my thrie fones, John, Thomas, and James; John Carftairs, Mr John Rollok, &c."<ref name=THDp84 /> In ''The Diary of Sir James Hope'', Balfour Paul notes that Sir Charles was generally known as Sir Charles Erskine of Cambuskenneth,<ref name=JPBp131>[[#JBP|Balfour Paul 1919]], p. 131.</ref> indeed Mary's father refers to her in his diary as Lady Cambufkynneth,<ref name=THDp135>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 135</ref> and L. Cambufkynneth<ref name=THDp191>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 191</ref> and her brother James refers to her as his sister Cambuskenneth.<ref name=JPBp131 />
'''Mary has a daughter''' On 15 November 1639, Marie was delivered of a maid child.<ref name=THDp111>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 111</ref>
'''Mary has a son''' On 1 May 1641, Lady Cambufkynneth was delivered of a man child. Mary's father gave Jean Cuningham, the midwife, one double angel.<ref name=THDp135 />
'''Sir Charles is a Commissioner and an MP''' At Edinburgh on 15 July, 1641, Lairds Bandeath and Polmaise (Sir William Murray of Touchadam and Polmaise) were Commissioners for the Barons for Stirling.<ref name=RPS /> Sir Charles was sworn in as a member of parliament on 17 August the same year.<ref>''The Records of the Parliaments of Scotland to 1707'', K.M. Brown et al eds (St Andrews, 2007-2015), A1641/8/1a. accessed: 10 March 2015. http://www.rps.ac.uk/trans/A1641/8/1a.</ref>
'''Daughter Marie dies''' On Friday, 17 June 1642, Mary's father wrote, "about 9 hours in the morning, my deir barne Marie Erfkin, (having lyen from 2 Junij in the pockis,) departed at 2 zieris and half-zeir aige." Mary was buried the following day in the Gray Frier yard.<ref name=THDp170>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 170-2</ref>
'''Son Thomas dies''' A few weeks later, Thomas recorded another loss in the family on 2 July 1642, "My deir oy Thomas Erfkin departit this lyff ..." having taken sic on the Thursday after his sweet sister Marie died ... "The Lord gif me grace to be patient and obedient to his holy will." Thomas Erskine was buried beside his sister Mary in the Grey Freirs, with their Hope great-grandparents.<ref name=THDp170 />
'''Mary has a son, son John dies''' The day after the burial of Mary's son, Thomas, between 3 and 4 in the afternoon on 4 July, Mary was safely delivered of one man child, baptised John by Mr Andro Ramfay on 5 July 1642, in memory of the Earl of Mar, his grandfather.<ref name=THDp170 /> On 26 September 1643, Thomas wrote "My oy John Erskin departit", his sweet babe John Erfkin was sick of a "defluction, and thair wes a cauter putt in his neck, be advyfe off Doctor Arnott; but he peynit an peynit ftill to the 5 of October 1643, ... at 6 in the morning; at quhilk tyme he departit." While the "barne" was sick, he was "erneft in prayer with my Lord" and sleeping while his wife helped attend the "barn".<ref name=THDp196>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], pp. 196-7</ref>
'''Son Charles is born''' On 12 June 1643, Sir Charles went to Leith to start his journey to France, with the Earl of Hadingtoun and William Erfkine, his brother german.<ref name=THDp189>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 189</ref> Mary was heavily pregnant when he left because several weeks later, on 1 July, Sir Thomas writes, "My dauchter L. Cambufkynneth delyverit of a man chyld about 7 hours in the morning, ... for quhilk I bliffe the Lord." The "barne baptiffit" on 4 July, 1643, by Mr William Colvin; witnessed by the Earl of Buchan, Scottiscraig, Mary's four brothers, and her father. Sir Thomas "held up the babe and namit him Charles, from his father being abfent in France."<ref name=THDp191 />
'''Sir Charles' mother dies''' Sir Charles came home from France on the "fevint" of May 1644. His mother, "Dame Marie Steuart, Countes of Mar", took a "deidlie brafche" on 1 May, 1644, dying ten days later at the house of Mary's father in the Cowgait. Her Ladyship's funeral was to be on Thursday, 23 May, at Alloway.<ref name=THDp205 /> Apparently, Charles was Lady Mar's favourite son and she had a residence at Sir Thomas Hope's where she frequently lodged - the house was still opposite St Magdalen's Chapel in the Cowgate in 1882.<ref>Fergusson, Alex, ''The honourable Henry Erskine, lord advocate for Scotland with notices of certain of his kinsfolk and of his time compiled from family papers and other sources of information '', 1882, W. Blackwood and Sons, Edinburgh, https://archive.org/stream/honourablehenrye00fergrich#page/20/mode/2up, p. 21, accessed 10 March, 2015.</ref>
'''Son James is born''' About two hours after midnight on 21 March, 1645, Mary was safely delivered of a man child. Mary's father Thomas wrote about this news: "Bleffit be the Lord, quho laidis us daylie with benefits."<ref name=THDp214>[[#THD|Hope 1843]], p. 214</ref> James Erskine's baptism on 25 March was witnessed by James Earl Buchan, James Hamiltoun, brother to the Earl of Hadingtoun, Mary's brother, Sir James, and James Steuart, Mr Mungo Law, minifter.<ref name=THDp214 />
Charles and Mary may have had more children, unfortunately, the diary of Charles' father-in-law, ends in 1645, and although his brother-in-law James' diary takes up in 1646, it ends about 10 years later and there are gaps.
'''Charles is a PC''' On 4 January, 1649, at Edinburgh, Parliament, there were many vacant places on the privy council because of deaths and depositions against some of them, so parliament nominated, elected and appointed ten replacement privy counsellors including Sir Charles Erskine of Alva, [Bandeath and Cambuskenneth]; knight, Robert [Arbuthnott], viscount of Arbuthnott; and Sir David Home of Wedderburn, knight.<ref>The Records of the Parliaments of Scotland to 1707, K.M. Brown et al eds (St Andrews, 2007-2015), 1649/1/430. accessed: 10 March 2015. http://www.rps.ac.uk/trans/1649/1/430</ref>
'''Charles remarries''' After the death of Mary, Charles married [[Skene-38|Helen Bruce nee Skene]], the daughter of [[Skene-39|Sir James Skene of Curriehill]], Midlothian, and widow of [[Bruce-1504|Robert Bruce of Broomhall]], Dunfermline, Fife.<ref name="pol">{{EE citation |first1=D W |last1=Hayton|title=''ERSKINE, John (1660-1733), of the Sand Haven, Culross, Fife.''|website=The History of Parliament: British Political, Social & Local History, Crown copyright and The History of Parliament Trust 1964-2014 |year=1964-2014 |accessdate=11 March 2015 |url=http://www.historyofparliamentonline.org/volume/1690-1715/member/erskine-john-1660-1733|repository= Web}}</ref>
'''Son John is born''' John Erskine, of the Sand Haven, Culross, Fife, MP, born September 1660, died 1733, was Charles' second surviving son and his second wife's first son.<ref name="pol" /> He married three times:#contract dated 17 April, 1682, Jean, the daughter of Murray of Polmiase, Stirling, who had 1 son and 2 daughters;<ref name="pol" />#on 29 April, 1697, Lady Mary, the daughter of George Maule, 2nd Earl of Panmure, and widow of Charles Erskine, 5th Earl of Mar, and they had no children,<ref name="pol" /> Mary's mother was Jean Campbelland the marriage was celebrated at Alloa, Clackmannan, Scotland.<ref>"Scotland, Marriages, 1561-1910," index, FamilySearch (https://familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/XTL2-JQ2 : accessed 10 March 2015), Charles Sir Erskine in entry for John Erskine and Mary Countess Of Marr Maule, 29 Apr 1697; citing Alloa,Clackmannan,Scotland, reference ; FHL microfilm 1,040,206.</ref> and #in 1714, Euphemia, the daughter of William Chochrane of Ochiltree, Ayr, and they had 3 daughters.<ref name="pol" />
Charles is the ancestor of the Baronet of Alva in the County of Fife, under the Baronetage of Nova Scotia which was granted to his son on 30 April 1666. The raising was a way of earning funds for the Crown and was purchased. A baronetcy is not a peerage, so baronets, like knights, are commoners as opposed to noblemen. The Baronage of Nova Scotia was used as a way of opening the lands in Nova Scotia and generally held a grant of land. These were often used by the families and settled by one of the junior sons. Unlike knighthood, which were applied only to an individual, a baronetcy is hereditary. The eldest son of a baronet who is born in wedlock succeeds to the baronetcy upon his father's death, but he will not be officially recognised until his name is on the Roll. With a few exceptions, granted at creation by special remainder in the Letters Patent, baronetcies can be inherited only by or through males. More on [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baronet Baronet] and the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_extant_baronetcies#Baronetage_of_Nova_Scotia_.281625.E2.80.931706.29 Baronetage of Nova Scotia].
He is noted, in the Parliamentary Records for his grandson, as being a vigorous opponent of Charles I and episcopacy.<ref>[http://www.historyofparliamentonline.org/volume/1690-1715/member/erskine-(areskine)-sir-john-1672-1739 The History of Parliament]</ref>
