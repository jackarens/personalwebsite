==Biography==Elisabeth was born in 1243, the daughter of Henry II, Duke of Brabant, and his second wife, Sophie of Thuringia.<ref name=Knetsch1>Knetsch, Carl. 1917. ''Das Haus Brabant: Genealogie der Herzog von Brabant und der Langrafen von Hessen''. Vol 1- Darmstadt: Vereins für das Grossherzogtum Hessen. p. 28 (image 41). Digital image. ''Philipps Universit��t, Marburg'' http://archiv.ub.uni-marburg.de/eb/2015/0060/view.html : viewed 20 January 2017.</ref>
Unsuccessful negotiations in 1247 to marry a daughter of Henry II to Edward (later Edward I), eldest son of Henry III, King of England, probably related to Elisabeth.<ref name=Knetsch1/> She did marry on 13 July 1254 at Braunschweig, while still a child, Albrecht I, Herzog von Braunschweig (Duke of Brunswick), though the marriage wasn't consummated until 1258.<ref name=Knetsch1/>
Elisabeth died childless either on 17 April or 9 October 1261 and was buried at Braunschweig.<ref name=Knetsch1/>
