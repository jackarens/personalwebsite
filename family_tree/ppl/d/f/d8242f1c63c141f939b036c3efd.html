== Biography ==
Alexander was born before 1413.<ref name="Cokayne">G.E. Cokayne; with Vicary Gibbs, H.A. Doubleday, Geoffrey H. White, Duncan Warrand and Lord Howard de Walden, editors, The Complete Peerage of England, Scotland, Ireland, Great Britain and the United Kingdom, Extant, Extinct or Dormant, new ed., 13 volumes in 14 (1910-1959; reprint in 6 volumes, Gloucester, U.K.: Alan Sutton Publishing, 2000), volume IX, page 134. </ref>He was the son of Sir John Montgomerie of Ardrossan and Agnes of the Isles,<ref name="Cokayne"> </ref> daughter of John of Islay, Lord of the Isles.<ref>MacQueen, Hector L. "Montgomery family". Oxford Dictionary of National Biography (online ed.). Oxford University Press. doi:10.1093/ref:odnb/54294 </ref>He was a Commissioner to negotiate with England in 1437.<ref name="Mosley"> Mosley, Charles, editor. Burke's Peerage, Baronetage & Knightage, 107th edition, 3 volumes. Wilmington, Delaware, U.S.A.: Burke's Peerage (Genealogical Books) Ltd, 2003. Volume 1, page 1279.</ref>
In 1444 Montgomerie was appointed keeper of Brodick Castle on the Isle of Arran.<ref> Lee, Sidney, ed. (1894). "Montgomerie, Alexander de". Dictionary of National Biography 38. London: Smith, Elder & Co. </ref>
Before 3 July 1445 he was created Lord Montgomerie.<ref name="Mosley"> </ref>
He married Margaret Boyd (who died after 16 September 1453), sister of Robert Boyd, 1st Lord Boyd, and 2nd daughter of Sir Thomas Boyd of Kilmarnock.<ref name="Cokayne2"> Cokayne, and others, The Complete Peerage, volume IX, page 135. </ref>
He was a Privy Councillor and took part in many embassies sent to England on behalf of the King.<ref name="Mosley"> </ref>
Alexander died in 1470<ref name="Cokayne2"> </ref> and was succeeded by he grandson Hugh Montgomerie, as 2nd Lord Montgomerie later 1st Earl of Eglinton.
