Narrative

"HENRY DE TRACY, Knt., of Barnstaple, Ashwater, Bovey Tracey, Bremridge (in South Molton), Fremington, High Bray, Little Bray, Martinhoe, Morthoe, Pickwell (in Georgeham), Pilton, West Anstey, West Down, etc., Devon, Constable of Exeter Castle, Governor of Lundy Island, Baron of the Exchequer, son and heir of Oliver de Tracy, of Barnstaple, Devon, by his wife, Eve, probable daughter of Fulk Fitz Warin, of Whittington, Shropshire and Westbury, Wiltshire. Her maritagium included the manor of Tawstock, Devon, which had previously been granted to her aunt, Lorette de Brewes, Countess of Leicester, in marriage. They had four sons, Henry, Thomas, Knt., John (clerk), and Oliver (clerk), and one daughter, Eve. He was heir in 1210 to his fathers one-half share of the barony of Barnstaple, Devon. He obtained the other half of the barony of Barnstaple in 1213, by gift of King John on the forfeiture of William de Brewes. He subsequently acquired the borough of Barnstaple by grant of King Henry III. He fought in Poitou in 1214, and joined the Barons against King John. He acquired a one-fifth share in the honour of Torrington, Devon by purchase from Richard Temenet. In 1221 he was sued by his brother-in-law, John de Brewes, in a plea of land. In 1228 the king commanded the sheriff of Devonshire to throw down Torrington Castle belonging to Henry de Tracy. He fought in France in 1230. In 1233 he was granted a weekly market at his manor of Ilfracombe, Devon. He was a justice itinerant in Cornwall in 1256. In 1259 he had a charter for free warren in his demesne lands of his manor of Tavistock, Devon, and the grant of a weekly market and yearly fair at his manor of Nymet Tracy, Devon. The same year he was ordered to pay Hervey de Chaworth 20 marks a year out of the 20l. a year of rent which he was bound to pay Patrick de Chaworth (Henrys brother-in-law) in Holsworthy, Devon, saving to Hawise widow of the said Patrick (Henrys half-sister) the remaining 10 marks a year for her dower. He joined the Barons against King Henry III. In 1260 commission was granted to him to view and test wines in all boroughs, cities and towns of the county of Devon. In 1263 he settled property in Argallas (in St. Enoder), Cornwall on his son, Thomas, and Thomas wife, Iseult, with remainder if Iseult have no issue to Henry and his heirs quit of the other heirs of Iseult. In 1265 he and his son, Oliver, were appointed Wardens of the sea shore in the county of Devon. SIR HENRY DE TRACY died shortly before 21 August 1274."
