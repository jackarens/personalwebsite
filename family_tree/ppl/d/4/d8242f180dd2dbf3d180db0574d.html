== Biography ==
Agnes, known variously as Ainé, Agnes, Margaret and Hannah, is identified as a daughter of Guy O'Cahan or O"Cathan. This might be a reference to Cuinnbhuighe O'Cathan who was probably the Conmuighe na Gall
son of Manus of the Battle of Downpatrick in 1260 A.D.
Agnes O’Cahan was first married to The O’Neill before Angus Og de Yle. She had an O’Neill son, Brian Balloch, to this first marriage and his half-brother Lord John and mother Agnes, supported Brian in an unsuccessful struggle for leadership against The O’Neill (Niall) who was backed by the “Mac Alaxandairs”.
Agnes was still alive in 1338: ''On a Chancery Roll, A.D. 1338, is copy of a safe-conduct, granted to Agnes, mother of John of the Isles, protecting her when crossing the Channel to and from Ireland, as often as she wished to do so.''<ref>Hill, page 17.</ref>
