==Biography==Malcolm Fleming is believed to have been born about 1312. He was the son of Sir Patrick Fleming of Biggar, second son of Robert Fleming of Cumbernauld, and his wife, stated to have been one of the two daughters and coheiresses of Sir Simon Fraser of Olivercastle in Peeblesshire, Sheriff of that county, who was executed by Edward I.. On account of this alliance the Fleming family thereafter quartered Fraser on their arms.<ref>[[#S-1]] Balfour Paul; Vol 8, [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun08pauluoft#page/524/mode/2up page 524]</ref> Balfour Paul, in his study of that family suggests her name as Joanna.<ref>[[#S-1]] Balfour Paul; Vol 7, [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun07pauluoft#page/422/mode/2up page 422]</ref>
===[[:Category:Battle of Neville's Cross|Battle of Neville's Cross]]===William Hunter provides<ref>[[#S-2]] William Hunter; [https://archive.org/details/biggarhouseoffle1867hunt#page/478/mode/2up page 478]</ref> that he was taken prisoner at the battle of Neville's Cross, 17 October 1346.

===Family Affairs===in 1357, he had a charter from King David of the lands of Lenzie which had been forfeited by John Kennedy, 11 January 1357. From his cousin Malcolm Fleming, Earl of Wigtown, he had a grant of the lands of Achmoir, Seymoir, and others 18 April 1357, and another charter to him and Christian his wife, under the Great Seal, of the lands of Lenzie, forfeited by John Kennedy 6 April 1361. This clearly suggests he was married prior to 1361 and likely well prior. His eldest son David Fleming is noticed as that beloved and faithful "bachellarius" David Fleming to whom King David II., on 27 October 1362, granted certain annual rents, from which we can assume he was of age. It is not clear why Christian was not included in the earlier charter of Lenzie.
Between 1364 and 1366 he was Sheriff of Dumbarton and keeper of the Royal castle there.
From William Boyd he had an assignation, to him and Christian his spouse, of the impignoration (pledge) which Thomas Fleming of Foulwood, formerly Earl of Wigtown, had made to him of the barony of Lenzie for 80 pounds sterling, dated at Cumbernauld the feast of St. Martin (11 Nov) 1372.
He had a charter of a tenement in Crawmond resigned by Marjorie Fleming, 16 January 1381, and another charter by King Robert II. of the barony of Lenzie resigned by Thomas Fleming, 20 September 1382.
He was Sheriff of Edinburgh in 1374 as he received boards for the repair of the Castle of Edinburgh.
Thomas, Lord of Foulwood, was alive about 1382 when Thomas conferred on him the barony of Lenzie, and this gift was confirmed by Robert II. on the 20th of September 1382. Malcolm must have inherited the titles to Biggar not long after this date.
His death is not noticed by Balfour Paul or William Hunter but he was seems to have been alive in 1392 when his son, David, is stated to be his son and heir. He was dead prior to 1399 when David, his son, mortified<ref>In Scots law, this was the act of disposing of lands for religious or charitable purposes.</ref> the lands of Lenzie thus assuring us they were held by him.

===Family===
He married a lady called Christian. They had two sons:
* Sir [[Fleming-323|David Fleming]]. He will inherit the titles as Lord of Biggar.
* [[Fleming-3811|Patrick Fleming]]. He, as second son of Malcolm Fleming, Lord of Biggar, had a charter on 17 April 1369 from Robert of Erskine, Knight, of all his lands in the barony of Leygnch, viz. the lands of Bord, Tweoures, Croy, Smithston, Balloch, and Ardrie in excambion (exchange) for the lands of Dalnotrie and Garscadden, in the earldom of Lennox, 17 April 1369, which was confirmed under the Great Seal 24 August 1373. He had a charter from his father of all his lands in the barony of Olivercastle, to wit his lands of Overmenzean and Glenrusco in Peeblesshire, on 7 November 1395, which was confirmed under the Great Seal 22 June 1397. He was the founder of the Flemings of Bord.

===Arms===His arms are said to be Fleming of Biggar quartering Fraser of Oliver Castle. That is; 1st and 4th argent, a chevron within a double tressure, flory and couter-flory glues for Fleming; 2nd and 3rd, azure three cinquefoils, argent for Fraser.
