== Biography ==John de Edmonstone (also known as Johanni de Edmondiston) is the son of [[Edmundiston-1|Henricus de Edmondiston]].<ref name=Edmonstone>Sir Archibald Edmonstone, ''Genealogical account of the family of Edmonstone of Duntreath'' (Edinburgh: privately printed, 1875), p. 20-23, digital images, https://books.google.com/books?id=mSoAAAAAQAAJ&pg=PA20. Google Books (http://books.google.com : accessed 9 August 2016).</ref> He died 7 April 1410.

He had the following children:<ref name=Edmonstone/>
# [[Edmonstone-13|Sir John Edmonstone]] (1325-1410)# [[edmonstone-83|Sir Archibald Edmonstone]] (bef. Edmonstoune, Scotland (Culloden, Inverness-shire, Scotland)1389-Strathblane, Stirlingshire, Scotland 1462).# [[Edmonston-9|Margaret]], married [[Sommerville-65|Sir John Sommerville of Carnworth]]
The last two children are considered questionable by Edmonstone in ''Genealogical account of the family of Edmonstone of Duntreath'' (1875).
