== Biography ==

Her year of birth is just a guess.
Warin de Bassingbourn "married before 1228 to '''Eve de Tracy''', daughter of Oliver de Tracy, baron of Barnstaple, Devon, by his wife, Eve, probably one of the daughters and co-heiresses of Matthew de Torrington [Richardson later identifies Eve as the [http://archiver.rootsweb.ancestry.com/th/read/GEN-MEDIEVAL/2008-12/1230700363 sister of Fulk FitzWarin]. Eve de Tracy's identity and parentage are proved by suits published in the Curia Regis Rolls. There is no question which Tracy family Eve de Tracy belongs to as she had the manor of Morebath, Devonshire given to her in marriage by her brother, Henry de Tracy. Also, in 1265, Eve's son, Sir Warine de Bassingbourne presented James de St. Victore, clerk, as rector of Morebath, Devon, proving that Eve was Sir Warin's mother [Reference:Rev. F.C. Hingeston-Randolph, Registers of Walter Bronescombe and Peter Quivil, Bishops of Exeter, published 1889, pg. 156].
"Eve de Tracy, wife of Warin de Bassingbourne, Sr., was kin to the Chaworth family, by virtue of her mother, Eve's second marriage to Thomas de London. Eve de Tracy's half-sister was Hawise de London, who married Patrick de Chaworth. This explains the appearance of the Chaworth, Tibetot and Muscegros witnesses, all of whom were near kinsmen of Warin de Bassingbourne the grantor. Maud de Chaworth, daughter and heiress of Patrick de Chaworth, later became the wife of Henry of Lancaster, Earl of Lancaster and Leicester (died 1345), the lineal ancestor of the Lancastrian kings of England."<ref>Douglas Richardson, [http://archiver.rootsweb.ancestry.com/th/read/GEN-MEDIEVAL/2002-08/1028698606 "CP Addition: Identity of Emme, wife of Laurence de Saint Maur"] posted at the gen-medieval forum in 2002.</ref>
