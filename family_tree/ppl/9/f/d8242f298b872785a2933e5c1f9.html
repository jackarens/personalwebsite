== Biography =='''Aline Burnel'''<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp421">{{EE citation |author=The Deputy Keeper of the Records |title=''Calendar of Inquisitions Post Mortem and other Analogous Documents preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=Hereford|publisher=His Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department |volume= Vol VI Edward II|year=1910|accessdate=3 October 2014|url=https://archive.org/stream/cu31924099427845#page/n459/mode/2up|repository=archive.org |pages=421}} Abstract No 685 Richard Hord of Walleford.</ref>
Aveline (Oliva) Despencer was born about 1266 or in 1288 in Southampton, Lincolnshire, England.
Aline's husband, Edward Burnel died at Thirnyngg in Norfolk on 22 August 9 Edward II.<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390">{{EE citation |author=The Deputy Keeper of the Records |title=''Calendar of Inquisitions Post Mortem and other Analogous Documents preserved in the Public Record Office'' |publication-place=London |publisher=His Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department |volume= Vol V Edward II|year=1908|accessdate=23 September 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/cu31924011387838#page/n417/mode/2up|repository=archive.org |pages=390-4}} Abstract No 611 Edward Burnel alias Bernel.</ref> On 1 September 9 Edward II [1315], a ''writ'' was issued regarding Edward Burnel<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390" /> and ''Inquisitions'' taken included:*at Gloucester on 10 March 9 Edward II [1316], which found that Edward held Little Rysindon jointly with his wife Aline, of the king in chief as of the honour of Wallyngford, by the gift and enfeoffment of Sir Ingelram Berynger when the honour was in the hands of Sir Peter de Gavaston then earl of Cornwall;<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390" />*at Salop on 28 October in 9 Edward II [1315], which found that Edward held jointly with his wife Aline, the manor of Eudon Burnel of Edmund earl of Arundell, and a moiety of the manor of Magna Ercaluwe of the gift and enfeoffment of William de Ercaluwe;<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390" />*at Norfolk on 4 November 1315, which found that Edward held jointly with his wife Aline, the manor of Riston of the gift of Ingelram Belunger, held of Robert Banyard, and the manor of Thynyngg held of John de Britannia, ear of Richmond;<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390" />*at Somerset on 28 November 1315, which found that Edward held jointly with his wife Aline, the manor of Compton Dauno of the king in chief, and land in Strete held of the enfeoffment of Nicholas de Eston of the abbot of Glastonberry;<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp390" />
Aline's father-in-law Philip Burnel, was the brother of Petronilla, who was married to William de Ercaluwe.<ref name="AntShropVolVIIIp37">{{EE citation|last=Eyton|first=Rev R W|title=''The Antiquities of Shropshire''|volume=Vol VIII|publication-place=London|publisher=John Russell Smith|year=1859|accessdate=2 October 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/antiquitiesshro06eytogoog#page/n53/mode/2up|repository=archive.org|pages=37}}</ref>
In 1321, Aline Burnel, widow of Edward Burnel, petitioned the King, that she might have for the term of her life, for a reasonable fine, the manor of Marley, which she purchased from Hugh Despenser the father without permission and which the escheator has seized.<ref name="SC81959741B">The National Archives Website: Discovery: SC 8/195/9741B, PETITIONS TO THE KING; TO THE KING AND COUNCIL; TO THE COUNCIL; TO THE PARLIAMENT; AND THE LIKE. http://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C9295629, 5 November 2014.</ref> In a letter dated 24 October, 15 Edward II (1321), the King, Edward II, wrote to William de Ayremeynne, enclosing the petition from Elaine (Alyne, Helen), widow of Edward Burnel, asking for his advice on what he could properly do.<ref name="SC81959741A">The National Archives Website: Discovery: SC 8/195/9741A, PETITIONS TO THE KING; TO THE KING AND COUNCIL; TO THE COUNCIL; TO THE PARLIAMENT; AND THE LIKE. http://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C9295628, 5 November 2014.</ref>
Aline Burnel, was living the widow of Edward Burnel in 19 Edward II [1326], when Richard Hord of Walleford was holding of her, for life by the courtesy of England, the hamlet of Chelmundewyk in Salop.<ref name="CalInqPMEdIIp421" />
On 10 July 14 Edward III [1340], the Lady Aline, widow of Edward Burnell, of the manor of Smethcote under the Longmynd, who held one third of the manor in dower of Lord John de handle, reached an agreement for the division of the woods and commons of the manor of Smethcote with the other people who held shares of that manor, ie, William de Smethcote; and John de Acton Scot and Rchard, son of Richard Tristremheld; the witnesses included Sir William de Erchalewe, Sir Roger Corbett de Cause, [[Burnell-190|Edward Burnell, Lord of Longleley]], and John du Lee, Lord of Biriton.<ref name="NAUK2922103112">The National Archives Website: Discovery: 2922/10/3/1/1-2, Manor of Smethcote, MANORIAL RECORDS. http://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/rd/a0d4eba8-d9a0-4d20-a4b7-fb97f19313d1, 5 November 2014.</ref>
Alina late the wife of Edward Burnel, knight, died on Tuesday before St Dunstan or 16 May, 1363.<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371">{{EE citation |author=The Deputy Keeper of the Records |title=''Calendar of Inquisitions Post Mortem and other Analogous Documents preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=London |publisher=His Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department |volume= Vol XI Edward III |year=1935|accessdate=3 October 2014|url=https://archive.org/stream/cu31924011387895#page/n405/mode/2up|repository=archive.org |pages=371-3}} Abstract No 489 Alina late the wife of Edward Burnel, knight.</ref>

''Inquisitions'' taken included those at:*Norfolk, which found she held the manors of Riston and Thirnyng for life by a fine levied in the king's court, with remainders to [[Haudlo-2|John de Haudlo]] for life then to [[Haudlo-9|Nicholas Burnel]] son of John de Haudlo who was deceased, and his heirs.<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371" />*Warwick, which found that Alina held the manors of Budeford and Brome for life of the inheritance of Edward Burnel, sometime her husband, with remainder to Nicholas Burnel son of John de Haudlo deceased, by a fine levied in the king's court, Nicholas Burnel is aged 30 and more, and her heir was Edward le Spenser, aged 24 years and more;<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371" />*Worcester, which found that she held the manor of Sockeleye for life by way of dower of the inheritance of Edward Burnel her late husband, with remainder to Nicholas de Burnel son of John de Haulowe; and properties in Bischleye, held for life by gift of Hugh le Despenser with remainder to Hugh and his heirs, who were Edward son of Edward le Despenser, kinsman and heir of the said Hugh, aged 26 and more.<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371" />*Gloucester, which found that Alina held the manor of Sobbury by gift of Hugh le Despenser with reversion to him and his heirs, so the reversion belongs to Edward le Despenser; and Little Rysyndon, which reverted to Nicholas Burnel.<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371" />*Salop, she held many properties including Eudon Burnell, Benthale, Whichecote, Chetyngdon, which all reverted to Nicholas Burnell.<ref name="CalInqPME3Vol11p371" />
Nicholas Burnel was the son of [[Burnell-7|Maud Burnel]], the sister of Aline's husband Edward, and Maud's second husband, John de Haulowe.
