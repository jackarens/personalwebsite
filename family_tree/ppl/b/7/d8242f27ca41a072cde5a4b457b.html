== Biography ==
'''Sir Thomas of Brotherton'''<ref name=Richardson>Douglas Richardson, ''[http://www.royalancestry.net/ Royal Ancestry: A Study in Colonial and Medieval Families],'' 5 vols., ed. Kimball G. Everingham (Salt Lake City, Utah: the author, 2013), Vol IV, pages 256-266, NORFOLK</ref>
:Thomas of Brotherton, 1st Earl of Norfolk, Lord Marshal of England, was the son of Edward I of England and his second wife, Margaret of France, who were married at Canterbury Cathedral, Kent, on September 8, 1299.<ref name=Richardson /><ref name=RAI64>Richardson's ''Royal Ancestry'' (Vol I, p 64)</ref> (For details about the life of Thomas, see his [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_of_Brotherton,_1st_Earl_of_Norfolk Wikipedia biography].)

=== Vitals ===
:'''Father''': King Edward of England<ref name=Richardson /><br>:'''Mother''': Margaret of France (1279-1318), daughter of Philippe III, King of France, and his 2nd wife, Marie, daughter of Henri III, Duke of Lorraine<ref name=RAI64 />:'''born''': June 1300 (1st or 2nd), Brotherton, Yorkshire, England<ref name=Richardson /><br>

:'''Wives''': # Alice de Hales, daughter of Sir Roger de Hale; '''married''' before January 8, 1326; and they had three children<ref name=Richardson /># Mary de Brewes, widow of Sir Ralph de Cobham and daughter of Sir Peter de Brewes and his wife Agnes de Clifford; '''married''' before April 4, 1336; no surviving children<ref name=Richardson />

:'''Died''' about September 20, 1338 and was buried in the choir of the Abby of Bury St. Edmunds. He left a will August 4, 1338.<ref name=Richardson /><br>
