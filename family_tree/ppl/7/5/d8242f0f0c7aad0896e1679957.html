== Biography ==
Patrick was born about 1330 as the second son of [[Fleming-493|Malcolm Fleming]], Lord of Biggar, and his wife Christian.<ref>[[#S-1]] Balfour Paul; Vol 8, [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun08pauluoft#page/524/mode/2up page 525]</ref>
Patrick, as second son of Malcolm Fleming, Lord of Biggar, had a charter on 17 April 1369 from Robert of Erskine, Knight, of all his lands in the barony of Leygnch, viz. the lands of Bord, Tweoures, Croy, Smithston, Balloch, and Ardrie in excambion for the lands of Dalnotrie and Garscadden, in the earldom of Lennox, 17 April 1369, which was confirmed under the Great Seal 24 August 1373. This would create the basis of the Barony of Bord.
On 7 Nov 1395, he received a charter from his father of all his lands in the barony of Olivercastle. These lands, were half the Fraser of Olivercastle lands inherited by his grandmother. The lands included the lands of Overmenzean and Glenrusco in Peeblesshire. This charter was confirmed under the Great Seal 22 June 1397.

He had died prior to 1420 and likely about 1410.

His wife is not noticed but he had:
* [[Fleming-3817|James Fleming]] of Bord; who renounced to his cousin [[Fleming-322|Malcolm Fleming of Biggar]] all his right in the lands of Overmenzean and Glenrusco (that is the Olivercastle lands) on 3 November 1421. There was clearly a family dispute at the time for Balfour Paul notes<ref>[[#S-1]] Balfour Paul; Vol 8, [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun08pauluoft#page/524/mode/2up page 525]</ref> that he remitted to the said Malcolm Fleming and his accomplices having art and part in the decease of the said Patrick Fleming, his father, all rancour he had conceived against him by reason of the said death. James had children of which, William Fleming of Bord is his heir.
