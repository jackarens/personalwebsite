== Biography =='''JOHN OF KENT, (Plantagenet'''-125), 3rd Earl of Kent, Lord Woodstock, 4th Lord Wake, 2nd son, born at Arundel, Sussex 7 April 1330. He married by dispensation dated 3 April 1348 '''ELIZABETH OF JULICH (or JULIERS'''), daughter of William (or Guillaume) I, Margrave (afterwards Duke) of Julich, Prince of the Empire, Earl of Cambridge in England, Lieutenant, Captain and Vicar General in France for King Edward III, by Jeanne, daughter of Guillaume III, Count of Hainault, Holland, and Zeeland [see HAINAULT 9.III for her ancestry]. She was the niece of Queen Philippe of Hainault, wife of King Edward III of England, They had no issue.
JOHN OF KENT, 3rd Earl of Kent, died 26 (or 27) Dec. 1352, and was buried in the Church of the Grey Friars, Winchester, Hampshire. Following his death, his widow, Elizabeth, was veiled at Waverley Abbey by Bishop Edington. In due course, being minded to change her condition, she married (2nd) at Wingham, Kent 29 Sept. 1360 '''EUSTACE (or EUSTACHE) D'AUBERCHICOURT (or DAUBRICHECOURT, DABRIDGECOURT),''' K.G., younger son of Nicholas D'Auberchicourt, Knt. They had two sons, Francois and William. SIR EUSTACE D'AUBERCHICOURT died shortly after 1 Dec. 1372. Lady Elizabeth of Julich, Countess of Kent, died 6 June 1411, and was buried in the Church of the Grey Friars, Winchester, Hampshire. She left a will dated 20 April 1411, proved 30 June 1411.
