== Biography ==
In 1328 Sir Edward de Keith received a charter to the lands of Kelly from King Robert the Bruce, ''witnessed by his brother Robert II Keith'', Marischal of Scotland.<ref> George Edward Cokayne, The complete Peerage; or, A history of the House of lords and all its members from the earliest times, Volume VIII, Ed. H. A. Doubleday & Howard de Walden (London: The St. Catherine Press, Ltd., 1932), p. 471</ref> Neither Sir Edward de Keith nor ''his brother Robert'' were at the Battle of Halidon Hill but his son William, fighting under Sir Archibald Douglas was taken prisoner there.<ref> George Edward Cokayne, The complete Peerage; or, A history of the House of lords and all its members from the earliest times, Volume VIII, Ed. H. A. Doubleday & Howard de Walden (London: The St. Catherine Press, Ltd., 1932), p. 471</ref>
