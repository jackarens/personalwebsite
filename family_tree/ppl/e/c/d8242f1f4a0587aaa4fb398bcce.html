== Biography ==
James (or John) was an Illegitimate son of an unknown mistress of King James II of Scotland. He was born illegitimately between 1445 and 1460. He obtained the lands of Stix, near Dunkeld, from Patrick Cardney, of Stixs, on 11 December, 1486,

In "the Heraldry of the Stewarts by George Harvey Johnston. Page 28
It clearly states the name as James Stewart of Sticks and Ballechin.But in the Peerage the apparently same Illegitimate son is refered to as John.

John (James) Stewart lived at Sticks, Scotland.

He died on 11 September 1523.

===Marriage===
He married Muriel Sutherland. She was the widow of Thomas Oliphant, of Dron, who had d. in 1484.

===Children===

Children of John (James) Stewart and Muriel Sutherland;

* 1. William Stewart, 2nd of Stix. d. without issue.

* 2. John Stewart, 3rd of Stix

* 3. James Stewart, d. at Edinburgh in 1508.

* 4. Patrick Stewart, 4th of Sticks,1st of Ballechin.
He was the ancestor of the Steuarts of Arnagang, Ballechin, Innervack, Killichassie, the later Kynachins, Loch of Clunie and Stewartfield.
