==Biography==

'''Father''' Sir John Beaufort, 1st Earl Somerset, Marquess of Dorset, Chamberlain of England b. c 1371, d. 16 Mar 1410

'''Mother''' Margaret Holand b. c 1385, d. 30 Dec 1439

Joan Beaufort was born in 1398. She married James I Stewart, King of Scotland, son of Sir Robert III Stewart, King of Scotland, Earl of Atholl & Carrick and Annabella Drummond, on 2 February 1424 at St. Mary Overy's, Southwark, Surrey, England; They had 2 sons (Alexander & James) and 6 daughters (Margaret, wife of Louis XI, King of France; Isabel, wife of François I, Duke of Brittay; Joan, wife of James Douglas, 1st Earl of Morton; Mary, wife of Sir Wolfart van Borsselen, Comte de Grandpré; Eleanor, wife of Sigismund von Tirol, Duke of Austria; & Annabelle, wife of Louis of Savoy, Count of Geneva, King of Cyprus, & of Sir George Gordon, 2nd Earl of Huntly).<ref>[S16] Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. I, p. 96-97.</ref><ref>[S16] Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. III, p. 577-578.</ref><ref>[S16] Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. IV, p. 40.</ref><ref>[S16] Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. IV, p. 103-104.</ref><ref>[S4] Douglas Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Vol. I, p. 655-656.</ref><ref>
[S4] Douglas Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Vol. IV, p. 644.</ref><ref>[S4] Douglas Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Vol. V, p. 41.</ref> Joan Beaufort and Sir James Stewart, Black Knight of Lorn obtained a marriage license on 21 September 1439; Dispensation to remain in marriage for being related in the 3rd & 3rd, 4th & 4th, and 3rd & 4th degrees of kindred and affinity. They had 3 sons (Sir John, 1st Earl of Atholl; Sir James, 1st Earl of Buchan; & Andrew, Bishop of Moray). Joan Beaufort died on 15 July 1445 at Dunbar Castle, Dunbar, East Lothian, Scotland; Buried beside her 1st husband (the King) in the Carthusian Convent, Perth, Perthshire.
