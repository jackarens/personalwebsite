== Biography ==

Robert Dictus Boyt (Boyd) born c. 1230 died c. 1290Robert Dictus Boyt (Boyd), son of Dominus Robertus, is first mentioned (1262) in a charter by Sir John Erskine, of the lands of Halkill, in which he is designated as "Robertus de Boyd, miles". He was probably born ABT 1230 in Noddsdale Water, Cunninghame, Ayrshire, Scotland. He took part in the Battle of Largs, in Ayrshire, October 3, 1263, between the Scots and King Haco (or Hakon), of Norway, for the possession of the western islands of Scotland. He is known in history as the hero of Goldberry Hill where According to the History of Rowallane: “Acho or Haakon, King of Norway, landit at Air with 160 schips and 20,000 men to claim land promised by Macbeth to his predecessors but not yet handed over”. The Rowallan account identifies the islands of Arran, Bute and the two Cumbraes as the disputed land. During this battle Sir Robert Boyd of Noddsdale (near Largs) and his company of soldiers put the enemy to flight at Goldberry Hill, south of the main engagement, near Portincross.The battle was a complete victory for the Scots and as a result of this victory the word "Goldberry" was placed on the Boyd Arms in commemoration of his services. He also received a grant of several lands in Cunninghame, Ayrshire, from King Alexander III. He died between 1272 and 1290 and it could be that he died in 1290, when during the invasion and occupation of Scotland by the armies of Edward I, the English committed an atrocity in Ayr by hanging a number of Scottish nobles. Sir Robert Boyd, the hero of Goldberry Hill may have been amongst the murdered men along with members of the Wallace family. He left a son: Robert de Boyt (Boyd). [1]
