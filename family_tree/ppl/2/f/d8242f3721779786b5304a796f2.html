:: :: Edmund was married twice, first on 8 April 1269 to Lady Aveline de Forz, the daughter of William de Forz, 4th Earl of Albemarle and Isabel de Reviers, Countess of Aumale. She died just 4 years after the marriage, at the age of 15, and was buried at Westminster Abbey. The couple had no children, though some sources believe she may have died in childbirth or shortly after a miscarriage.

===Death and burial of Aveline, wife of Edmund, Earl of Lancaster===(Royal Tombs of Medieval England) In1268 Henry III's son, Edmund, Earl of Lancaster, was granted royal permission to marry Isabella Redvers, the widow of William de Forz, Earl of Aumale, but the following year married her ten-year-old daughter, Aveline, instead. Aveline died on 10 November 1274 and was buried north of the high altar at Westminster. As early as the 1290's, the tombs of Henry III's son, Edmund, Earl of Lancaster, and his wife, Aveline de Forz, were erected outside the Confessor's Chapel to the north of the Westminster high altar, a position of considerable sanctity, but one removed from the most sacred relics, suggesting considerations of seniority. Aveline's tomb stands today in the north-west bay of the Sanctuary, and features a freestone effigy, arcaded Purbeck tomb-chest, and single-arched canopy. The tomb probably stood originally in the center of the bay, and was moved westward to accommodate the tomb of Aymer de Valence (d.1274).
