== Biography ==
George was born before 1378<ref>G.E. Cokayne; with Vicary Gibbs, H.A. Doubleday, Geoffrey H. White, Duncan Warrand and Lord Howard de Walden, editors, The Complete Peerage of England, Scotland, Ireland, Great Britain and the United Kingdom, Extant, Extinct or Dormant, new ed., 13 volumes in 14 (1910-1959; reprint in 6 volumes, Gloucester, U.K.: Alan Sutton Publishing, 2000), volume I, page 154. </ref> the illegitimate son of William Douglas, Earl of Douglas & his mistress Margaret Stewart, (his wife's sister-in-law), Countess of Angus. George was the first Earl of Angus in the Douglas family, inheriting the title from his mother.
He was the first husband, (contract 24 May 1397), of Mary Stewart, daughter of King Robert Stewart III<ref>Alison Weir, Britain's Royal Families: The Complete Genealogy (London, U.K.: The Bodley Head, 1999), page 228. </ref>. [http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/SCOTTISH%20NOBILITY.htm#GeorgeDouglas1Angusdied1402B Foundation for Medieval Genealogy]
He was created 1st Earl of Angus on 9 April 1389.<ref name="Mosley">Mosley, Charles, editor. Burke's Peerage, Baronetage & Knightage, 107th edition, 3 volumes. Wilmington, Delaware, U.S.A.: Burke's Peerage (Genealogical Books) Ltd, 2003. Volume 1, page 1281.</ref>
He fought in the Battle of Homildon Hill on 14 September 1402, where he was captured by the English. He died after September 1402 in Lancashire, England from the Bubonic Plague.<ref name="Mosley"> </ref>,<ref>G.E. Cokayne; with Vicary Gibbs, H.A. Doubleday, Geoffrey H. White, Duncan Warrand and Lord Howard de Walden, editors, The Complete Peerage of England, Scotland, Ireland, Great Britain and the United Kingdom, Extant, Extinct or Dormant, new ed., 13 volumes in 14 (1910-1959; reprint in 6 volumes, Gloucester, U.K.: Alan Sutton Publishing, 2000), volume I, page 155. </ref>
He has an extensive biographical entry in the Dictionary of National Biography.<ref> Matthew H.C.G., editor, Dictionary of National Biography on CD-ROM (Oxford, U.K.: Oxford University Press, 1995). </ref>
