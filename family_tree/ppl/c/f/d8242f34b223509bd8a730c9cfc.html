== Biography ==
: Roger la Zouche, son of Alan la Zouche and Ellen de Quincy, was born about 1240/42. <ref name="MCA">'' Magna Carta Ancestry: A Study in Colonial and Medieval Families''</ref> Roger's father, Sir Alan La Zouche (Born about 1203) had the whole county of Chester, and all North Wales placed under his government. In the 46th year of Henry III, he was made Justice Itinerant for the cos. of Southampton, Buckingham, and Northampton. He was constituted Constable of the Tower of London, and Governor of the castle at Northampton.
: Alan was violently assaulted in Westminster Hall, 19 June 1270, by John de Warenne, 7th Earl of Surrey (born 1231), upon occasion of a dispute between them regarding some landed property, and was mortally wounded. His son, Roger, who was with him, was severely wounded. Roger succeeded to the barony of la Zouche 20 October 1270, after the death of his father, Alan, 10 August, 1270. <ref> ''Dictionary of National Biography, 1885-1900''</ref>
: Roger la Zouche; b. c 1240-42; m. by 1267 Ela (d. by 19 July 1276), daughter of Stephen Longespee, the son of William Longespee Earl of Salisbury (illegitimate son of Henry II), and d. just prior to 15 Oct 1285. [Burke's Peerage]
: Sir Roger la Zouche, b. 1240-2, d. shortly bef. 15 Oct 1285, Lord Zouche of Ashby and Brockley; m. bef. 1267 Ela Longespee. [Magna Charta Sureties]
: Roger le Zouche m. Ela, dau. and co-heir of Stephen de Longespée, 2nd son of William, Earl of Salisbury, and dying in 1285, was s. by his son, Alan la Zouche. [Sir Bernard Burke, Dormant, Abeyant, Forfeited, and Extinct Peerages, Burke's Peerage, Ltd., London, 1883, p. 598, Zouche, Baron Zouche, of Ashby, co. Leicester]
