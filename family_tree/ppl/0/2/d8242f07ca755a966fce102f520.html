== Biography ==

===Parentage===
Price states that Gwenllian ferch Llywelyn was the daughter of [[ap Iorwerth-26|Llywelyn ap Iorwerth]] and his mistress [[ferch_Llywarch-3|Tangwystl ferch Llywarch]] and the sister of Gruffydd ap Llywelyn.<ref name="Acts"/>
This is also stated by Marriages and politics in Wales, 1066-1282. <ref name="Marriages">"Marriage and politics in Wales, 1066-1282" ''Welsh History Review'' 4 Nos. 1-4 (1968-69):3-20, especially 17; OCR copy, Welsh Journals Online. (http://welshjournals.llgc.org.uk/browse/viewpage/llgc-id:1073091/llgc-id:1074521/llgc-id:1074544/getText : accessed 26 Feb 2016).</ref>
Wikipedia shows Gwennlian's mother to be Eleanor de Montfort, but this is an error. Eleanor married Llywelyn ap Gruffydd, Gwenllian's nephew by her brother Gruffydd.<ref name="Marriages"/>)
Cawley states that only Gruffydd is known to be Tangwystl's child and therefore Gwenllian, birth date unknown, was born to Llywelyn and a mistress other than Tangwystl. <ref name="fmg"> [http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/WALES.htm#Llywellyndied1240B Llywelyn ap Iorwerth], Cawley, The Foundation for Medieval Genealogy </ref>

===Birth Estimation===
No sources give a birth year for Gwennlian. If she was 15 at her first marriage in 1224, her birth year could be estimated at 1209. This year is consistent with other estimates.

===1224 Marriage to William de Lacy===
By 28 July 1224, she had married William de Lacy, son of Hugh de Lacy and Rose O'Connor of Connacht (daughter of Roderick O'Connor), and half-brother of Hugh de Lacy, Earl of Ulster.<ref name="Lloyd">John Edward Lloyd, "Who Was Gwenllian de Lacy," ''Archaeologia Cambrensis'' 19 (1919): 292-298. Image copy, HathiTrust http://babel.hathitrust.org/cgi/pt?id=uiug.30112051008636;view=1up;seq=304 : 2016</ref>
In his report to King Henry dated 5 Aug 1224 following the capture of castle Trum, William Marshall Earl of Pembroke records that "the wife of William de Lacy, the daughter of Llewllyn, Griffin´s sister, the wife of Thomas Blund, William´s brother, and the mother of William, of Thomas, and of him that was killed" were in the castle[295]. <ref name="fmg"/>
The fact of this married is confirmed by the case she brought in 1241 against Dafyd ap Llywelyn for the recovery of manors.<ref name="Acts">''The acts of Welsh rulers, 1120-1283'' by Dr. Charles Insley Huw Pryce, Cardiff : University of Wales Press, 2005. Accessed 25 Feb 2016 at [https://books.google.com/books?id=4E2uBwAAQBAJ&pg=PA412&lpg=PA412&dq=william+de+lacy+llywelyn&source=bl&ots=6A-KMf1cu7&sig=QLy3KRlodXyGbgVs7IiZ5Zz_MQI&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwib15uw6ZXLAhVN-mMKHYbxDMAQ6AEIMjAF#v=onepage&q=william%20de%20lacy%20llywelyn&f=false GoogleBooks], Page 412</ref>
m William de Lacy, son of HUGH de Lacy & his second wife Rose --- (-killed in battle Monach-cranncain 1233).<ref name="fmg"/>
William died in 1233. There were apparently no children from this marriage.

===1280 Death of Gwennlian===
She died between 30 Aug 1280 and 14 Nov 1281.<ref name="Acts"/><ref name="Lloyd"/>
