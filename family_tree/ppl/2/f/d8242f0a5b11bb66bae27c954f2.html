==Biography==
Entered by Katherine Wall, Sunday, October 20, 2013.</ref>
On 7 May, 1250, licence was given for Margery, late the wife of Theobald le Butiler, to marry as she willed, and a mandate sent to John son of Geoffrey, justiciary of Ireland, to permit her to marry as she willed, and to let her have reasonable terms for the fine as expedient for the king.<ref name="PatentsHenIIIp65">{{EE citation|editor1-last= Assistant Keeper of HM Public Records|title=''Calendar of the Patent Rolls Preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=London|publisher=His Majesty's Stationery Office|volume=Henry III 1247-1258|year=1908|accessdate=10 October 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/patentrollsreig00unkngoog#page/n76/mode/2up|pages=65}}</ref>

Margaret died in 1251.<ref name="KW" />
On 1 March, 1253, Peter de Chauvent, king's yeoman, was granted the marriage of Margery, late the wife of Theobald le Butiller, so that if she paid a fine to marry who she wanted to marry, the fine was paid to de Chauvent.<ref name="PatentsHenIIIp180">{{EE citation|editor1-last= Assistant Keeper of HM Public Records|title=''Calendar of the Patent Rolls Preserved in the Public Record Office''|publication-place=London|publisher=His Majesty's Stationery Office|volume=Henry III 1247-1258|year=1908|accessdate=10 October 2014 |url=https://archive.org/stream/patentrollsreig00unkngoog#page/n192/mode/2up|pages=180}}</ref>
