== Biography ==Otes de Holand, K.G., son of Robert de Holand, Knt., by his wife Maude la Zouche (Robert and Maude married before 1309/10).<ref>[[#Richardson|Richardson]]: Vol 2, pp 392-5</ref>
Sir Otho, or Otes Holand, was a younger son of Robert lord Holand, by Maud de la Zouche, and brother to Thomas earl of Kent, also one of the Founders of the Order.
In 1350, he incurred the displeasure of the king by his conduct in the following transaction The count of Eu constable of France having surrendered at Caen in 1346 to his elder brother Sir Thomas Holand king Edward purchased that nobleman from the latter and delivered him by an indenture into the custody of Sir Otho Holand under condition that the prisoner should not be permitted to leave England or to wear arms publicly until he should have paid his full ransom to the king It seems notwithstanding that Sir Otho took the count with him to Calais where he was seen at large and armed Information thereof being given sir Otho was brought to the bar of the king's bench before the chancellor and other high personages and being unable to deny the charge he put himself upon the king's favour and was thereupon committed to the custody of the marshal.
He accompanied his brother Thomas, in 1355, into Britanny; and it was probably in the course of this expedition that he was made prisoner, together with sir Thomas Beaumont near Grandserre in [[Wikipedia:Dauphin%C3%A9|Dauphiny]]. He appears to have been governor of the Channel islands in 1359.4 A fine was acknowledged in the same year between our diff querent and his brother sir Robert de Holand the elder and Robert de Holand the younger deforcients of the manor of Yoxhall in Staffordshire to the use of sir Otho for life with remainder to the deforcients and their heirs.
He died soon afterwards, 3rd September 1359, in Normandy, without issue; and his lands passed, under different entails, to his brothers Robert and Thomas lords Holand.
