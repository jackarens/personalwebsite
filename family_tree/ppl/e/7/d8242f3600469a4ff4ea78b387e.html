== Biography ==
'''Father''' Sir Ingelram (Enguerrand) de Fiennes, Seigneur de Fienes b. c 1204, d. c Oct 1270

'''Mother''' Isabel d. 9 Sep 1296

Maud de Fiennes was born circa 1254; A near kinswoman to Eleanor of Provence and to Eleanor of Castile.<ref>Douglas Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Vol. I, p. 417-418</ref>
A contract for the marriage of Maud de Fiennes and Sir Humphrey VII de Bohun, 7th Earl Hereford & 8th Earl Essex, Constable of England was signed between 15 June 1275 and 20 June 1275.<ref>Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. I, p. 237-238</ref>
Maud de Fiennes married Sir Humphrey VII de Bohun, 7th Earl Hereford & 8th Earl Essex, Constable of England, son of Sir Humphrey VI de Bohun and Eleanor de Brewes, before 20 July 1275; They had 1 son (Sir Humphrey, 8th Earl Hereford, 9th Earl Essex) & 1 daughter (Maud, possibly married to a member of the Baskerville family). He also had an illegitimate son (John de Hereford, a cleric).<ref>Douglas Richardson, Plantagenet Ancestry, p. 120-121.</ref>
Maud de Fiennes died on 6 November 1298; Buried at Walden Abbey, Essex.
