==Biography==
'''Father''' Sir Robert de Holand, 1st Lord Holand, Sheriff of Flint, Justice of Chester<ref>Douglas Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd Edition, Vol. II, p. 392-394</ref> b. c 1283, d. 7 Oct 1328
'''Mother''' Maud la Zouche<ref>Douglas Richardson, Plantagenet Ancestry, p. 398.</ref> b. c 1290, d. 31 May 1349

Maud de Holand, born circa 1310, was the daughter of Sir Robert de Holand, 1st Lord Holland, and his wife Maud la Zouche.<ref name="cit4">Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd edn., Volume 3, pages 488-489</ref><ref name="cit3">Richardson, Magna Carta Ancestry, 2nd edn., Volume 2, page 396</ref>
She married (1st) Sir John de Mowbray, 3rd Baron Mowbray.<ref name="cit14">Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Volume 4, page 183</ref> Apparently Maud and Sir John and his mother Aline were promptly arrested (26 February 1321/2) and taken to the Tower of London.<ref name="cit14"/> The marriage was subsequently voided.<ref name="cit4"/><ref name="cit3"/>

She married (2nd) Sir Thomas de Swinnerton, 3rd Lord Swinnerton.
They had 3 sons (Sir Robert, 4th Lord Swinnerton; William; and Roger).<ref name="cit4"/><ref name="cit3"/> They also had a daughter Alice, wife of Sir John de Gresley.<ref>The Royal Descents of 500 Immigrants, by Gary Boyd Roberts, p. 350</ref>
Maud de Holand was apparently dead by 10 May 1380, and was buried in the church of Swinnerton, Staffordshire.<ref name="cit4"/>
