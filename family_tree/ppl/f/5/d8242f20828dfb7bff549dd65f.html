== Burial ==(Royal Tombs of Medieval England) He died at Wolvesey Place, Winchester on 26 March 1447 (Richardson in Royal Ancestry says April) and was buried in the chantry-chapel of the retro-choir in Winchester Cathedral, Winchester, Hampshire, as instructed by his will dated the previous year. A drawing of his coffin made in the early 16th century shows Henry Beaufort's coffin draped in cloth of gold with a white cross and bearing a funeral effigy complete with mitre and cardinal's hat. There are no records for his monument, which was almost certainly planned before his death. His 1446 will records that the position of the tomb had already been chosen, and that the tomb was to be enclosed by a chapel with three monks celebrating mass three times daily for his family.
The bishop's monument was erected to the south of the Winchester retrochoir, and immediately south of the position selected for the new shrine of St. Swithun. Beaufort's chantry is freestanding within the Winchester retrochoir. Henry Beaufort's tomb today comprises a painted, wooden effigy, effigy-plinth, and heraldic Purbeck marble tomb-chest. The effigy, depicting the bishop wearing a cardinal's hat and robes, with hands clasped in prayer is not original. An account made in 1635 records the bishops image fashioned in wood and 'Brasse', suggesting an effigy of copper-plate. The Winchester effigy was most likely stripped of metal by Cromwell's Parliamentarian troops in 1642. Tomb-chest metal decorations were probably also stripped by Cromwell's troops in 1642 and were replaced sometime after 1789.
