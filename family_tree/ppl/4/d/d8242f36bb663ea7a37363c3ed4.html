== Biography ==
* Name at Birth: Isabella Warenne, born in Warren, Sussex, England on 23 Sept, 1256; died in Arundel Castle on 23 Oct, 1295. Baroness of Bywell, wife of William le Scot de Balliol and John de Balliol, King of Scots. Mother of Lord William of Galloway Ball and others. Her parents appear to be John Planagent de Warenne and Alice de Lusignan (Plantagent de Warenne).
* Father: John, 6th Earl of Surrey de Warenne, 1231 - 1305* Mother: Alice, Countess of Surrey de de Lusignan (Warenne), 1224 - 1255
* Spouse: John I de Balliol, King of Scotland, 1249-1314* Siblings: Alice de Warenne, 1248 - ?; Sir William Planagenet 5th Earl of Surrey de Warenne, 1256 - 1286; Eleanor de de Warenne (Percy), 1251 - 1282* Children: Anne de Baliol, 1276 - 1302; Henry Baliol, 1285 - 1332; Margaret Balliol
* FMG notes (http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/SCOTLAND.htm) specifically that John II and Isabella had two sons. First son Edward Balliol (1283-1367) would become King of Scotland. Ref (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Balliol). Documentation is weak regarding a second son Henry Balliol Regardless, Henry was killed in the battle at Annon on 16 Dec 1332, leaving no children.
* Source states that John Balliol "m (before 7 Feb 1279) ISABEL de Warenne, daughter of JOHN de Warenne Earl of Surrey & his [second wife ---] ([1265/69]-). The Chronicle of Thomas Wykes records the marriage ���infra octavus Purificationis” in 1279 of “Johannes de Balhol” and “adolescentulam Isabellam filiam comitis Warennæ”[883]. The word “adolescentulam” suggests her birth in [1265/69]. If that is correct, it appears that Isabel must have been born from an otherwise unrecorded second marriage of her father. This would also be more consistent with the date of death of her son Edward, who would have been extremely old if born soon after his parents’ marriage."
