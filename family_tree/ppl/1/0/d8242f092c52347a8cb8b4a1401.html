== Biography of William Abernethy ==
William was born about 1220. He is the third son of [[Abernathy-222|Laurence de Abernethy]] and [[Unknown-98584|Devorguile]].<ref>[[#S-1]] Sir James Balfour Paul; [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun07pauluoft#page/398/mode/2up Vol. 7, page 399]</ref> His date of birth is an estimate.

He will acquire the lands of Saltoun in East Lothian.
He appears in 1273, in a Charter where he grants two merks out of the profits of his mill at Ulkestone (Oxton), in Lauderdale, to the Abbey of Dryburgh.
History suggests he was involved in the murder of the Earl of Fife, in 1288, and may have been imprisoned in Douglas Castle. Sir James Balfour Paul argues this as unlikely as, if this was the case, he was probably dead before 1291, otherwise he would in all likelihood have been included in the order transferring his brother, Sir Hugh, to the royal prison.<ref>[[#S-1]] Sir James Balfour Paul; [https://archive.org/stream/scotspeeragefoun07pauluoft#page/398/mode/2up Vol. 7, page 399]</ref>
He is known to have been dead prior to 1296 as his widow, Margaret, but whose surname is unknown, then received a pension from the family estates by order of Edward I..
