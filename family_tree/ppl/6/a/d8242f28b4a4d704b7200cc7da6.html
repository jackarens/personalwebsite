'''Political Intrigue'''<br/> Jeanne's husband, Robert III of Artois, was in line to inherit the lands of Artois. When [[D'Artois-29|Robert's grandfather]] died, the lands and title went to Robert's [[De_Artois-12|Aunt Mahaut]], daughter of Robert's grandfather. Robert attempted to wrest this inheritance away from Mahaut and later her daughter on several occasions. Mahaut and her daughter both died suddenly. Poisoned? A disgruntled ex-employee of Mahaut, Jeanne de Divion claimed to have seen papers that would prove Robert's right to the inheritance. Unable to actually produce the papers, forgeries were made and produced in court. When the forgeries were discovered, [[Valois-119|King Philippe VI]], half-brother of Jeanne, Robert's wife, was furious. Robert fled and was banished. He eventually ended up in the Court of King Edward of England, who was the chief enemy of Philippe. Jeanne (Valois) d'Artois was not innocent in these schemes. Despite the fact that she was the king's half sister, in 1334, Philippe imprisoned her and her sons in the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_Gaillard Chateau Gaillard].<ref>Jervis, William Henley. ''A History of France: From the Earliest Times to the Establishment of the Second Empire in 1852'' ([https://books.google.com/books?id=G18AAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA199#v=onepage&q&f=false Google eBook]) Harper & brothers, 1882.</ref> The sons were eventually released.

'''Death'''<br/> Jeanne de Valois died on 9 July 1363.<ref name="RA1" /> Many texts state that she remained imprisoned in Normandy until her death.<ref name="ML" /><ref>[http://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/CAPET.htm#JeanneValoisdied1363 MEDIEVAL LANDS]: A prosopography of medieval European noble and royal families.</ref> Others place her death at Paris, Ile-de-France, France.<ref name="RA1">Douglas Richardson, Royal Ancestry, Vol. I, p. 146.</ref>
Jeanne de Valois was buried beside the main altar of the "Augustins de Paris." Her tomb of black marble, containing an epitaph, was later transferred to another area of the church.<ref>[https://archive.org/stream/bub_gb_n9lEAAAAcAAJ#page/n403/mode/1up ''Histoire genealogique et chronologique de la maison royale de France.''] by Anselme de Sainte-Marie. 1726</ref><ref>This is not the current Church of St. Augustine in Paris, which was not built until the 1870's.</ref>

*Christine is another daughter found in some genealogies, for whom there seems to be no valid sources.
